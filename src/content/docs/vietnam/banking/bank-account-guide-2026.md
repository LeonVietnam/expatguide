Most online guides about banking in Vietnam still describe processes that worked in 2019. The loopholes are closed. In 2026, the system is tied tightly to your visa status and enforced by a national biometric identity mandate that froze tens of millions of accounts in 2025.

This guide covers what you actually need to open an account, configure it correctly, and keep it from being locked.

---

## Can I Open a Bank Account in Vietnam? (Quick Answer)

Your eligibility depends almost entirely on your visa status. Here is where most expats stand:

**Tourist Visa or 90-day E-Visa:** Practically no. Major banks — Vietcombank, BIDV, Techcombank, HSBC — will not open accounts for tourist visa holders as of 2026. Digital banks like Timo (BVBank) may open a heavily restricted account tied to your visa, but it will not issue a physical ATM card, will not allow cash deposits, and will be automatically suspended when your visa lapses. This is a last resort, not a banking strategy.

**Business Visa or Short-Term Visa (no TRC):** Very limited. You are unlikely to qualify at any major branch. Some smaller local banks (TPBank, ACB, MB Bank) may make exceptions at individual branch discretion, but the resulting account is restricted: payment-only, no interest, no outbound international transfers. Success rate is low and declining.

**Temporary Residence Card (TRC) with Work Permit or Investor Visa:** Yes, full access. This is the standard path for long-term expats. It unlocks full-featured accounts at all major banks with a physical debit card, mobile banking, and standard international transfer capabilities.

**One important 2026 update:** A recent State Bank of Vietnam regulation has formally removed the blanket 12-month residency requirement and now allows banks to issue cards tied to a foreigner's remaining legal stay. In practice, most major banks still use 12 months as their internal filter, but this may become more flexible as the policy is absorbed.

## The 2026 Biometric Mandate: What It Means for Foreigners

This is the single most important update to understand before you open an account.

Under Decision 2345/QĐ-NHNN, any online transfer exceeding 10 million VND (approximately $390 USD) per transaction — or 20 million VND cumulative in a single day — requires biometric face authentication inside your banking app. This took effect on July 1, 2024, and full enforcement began January 1, 2025.

For Vietnamese citizens, this works by matching a live face scan against biometric data stored on their chip-based national ID card (CCCD). Foreign residents do not have a CCCD, so the standard in-app flow will fail, blocking your ability to pay rent, make large purchases, or transfer money.

**The practical fix for foreigners:** When opening your account, you must complete a manual biometric registration at the branch. Tell the teller you need to register your biometrics as a foreign national. They will scan your passport and complete the registration on the bank's internal system, or walk you through registering a VNeID Level 1 or Level 2 account (the Vietnamese digital identity system, now extended to foreign nationals). Do not leave the branch until this step is confirmed and you have tested a transfer in the app.

## How to Open an Account: Step by Step

This process assumes you hold a valid TRC and Work Permit (or qualifying Investor Visa).

**Step 1 — Prepare your documents**

Bring originals of everything. Banks will photocopy and stamp on-site, but will reject you without originals present:

- Passport (6+ months remaining validity)
- Temporary Residence Card (TRC)
- Work Permit or signed labor contract
- *Tạm Trú* certificate (police residence registration)
- Vietnamese phone number (essential for OTP and app activation)

If you hold an Investor Visa without a Work Permit, requirements vary by bank and sometimes by branch. Call ahead.

**Step 2 — Go to a main city branch, not a local office**

Staff at district-level head branches deal with foreign compliance paperwork regularly. Neighborhood branches may simply turn you away due to unfamiliarity with the documents. In Ho Chi Minh City, target branches in District 1 or Thảo Điền. In Hanoi, target branches in Tây Hồ or Hoàn Kiếm.

**Step 3 — Bring a Vietnamese speaker if possible**

The biometric registration and compliance forms involve terminology that does not translate cleanly. A Vietnamese-speaking colleague or friend will cut the process time significantly and reduce the risk of errors on forms.

**Step 4 — Request the right card**

Do not accept the default domestic NAPAS card. It cannot be used for international online transactions. Explicitly request a **Visa or Mastercard Debit**. Confirm this before signing anything.

**Step 5 — Set up Smart OTP before you leave**

Ask the teller to activate **Smart OTP** in your banking app immediately, and disable SMS OTP. SMS OTP requires your Vietnamese SIM to have mobile signal. If you travel outside Vietnam, roam on a foreign SIM, or your local SIM data lapses, SMS OTP will lock you out of your account. Smart OTP works over Wi-Fi anywhere in the world.

**Step 6 — Save your branch contact**

Ask for the Zalo contact of the teller or account manager who opened your account. If your account is flagged later, or you need to update your passport number when it expires, a direct message to your contact at the branch is far more efficient than returning to the queue.

## Which Bank Should You Use?

No single bank is best for every expat. Here is how the main options compare:

**Techcombank** — Best overall for digital nomads and long-term residents. Their app consistently ranks as the most stable and user-friendly. Zero-fee domestic transfers. English interface available. Requires Work Permit, labor contract, and visa or TRC.

**Vietcombank** — Best for ATM access. The largest branch and ATM network in Vietnam, accepted by every payment gateway and e-wallet (MoMo, ZaloPay, VNPay). Service can be slow and bureaucratic, but the account works everywhere. Requires passport with 6+ months validity and long-term visa.

**Shinhan Bank (South Korean)** — Favored by the expat community for English-language service and lower fees than Western banks. Requires TRC valid for at least 12 months. Good choice if you prefer dealing with international-standard staff.

**HSBC / Standard Chartered** — Best for large international transfers and cross-border business. English support is the strongest here. HSBC Premier requires either a 1 billion VND average monthly balance or a 100 million VND monthly salary — out of reach for most individuals. Worth considering if your employer banks with them or you handle significant remittances.

**TPBank / MB Bank** — Best digital experience among local banks. Both offer English-language apps, 24/7 self-service kiosks (TPBank LiveBank), and eKYC onboarding. More likely to work with shorter visa durations than the larger state banks, though still subject to compliance review.

**Timo (BVBank)** — Only viable for tourists or those between visa statuses. Opens with any valid visa. Functionality is severely limited: no outbound international transfers, no interest-bearing accounts, account tied directly to visa validity. Use only as a stopgap.

## Operational Pitfalls to Avoid

**The TRC expiration freeze**

Your account status is linked directly to your TRC expiration date. When your visa expires, the bank's compliance system will automatically restrict or freeze the account. Withdraw sufficient cash at least one week before your visa expires to cover living expenses while you wait for renewal. Do not assume you can access your account while renewal is in progress.

**The signature mismatch**

Vietnamese bank branches rely on exact handwritten signature verification for in-person withdrawals. If your signature varies even slightly from the original form you signed when opening the account, you may be refused. Sign consistently and photograph your signature for reference.

**The cash deposit restriction**

You cannot deposit cash at the counter without documentation proving the legal origin of the funds. Branches typically require a stamped labor contract or official payroll records. Vietnamese bank accounts for foreigners are primarily designed to receive wire transfers and salary deposits — not to bank physical cash.

**The NAPAS-only trap**

If you were given a NAPAS-only card and did not notice, it will not work on international websites like Google, Airbnb, or Booking.com. Check your card network before leaving the branch. If issued a NAPAS card by mistake, return and request a Visa or Mastercard debit card to be issued in its place.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Wise a substitute for a Vietnamese bank account?**

It is a useful supplement, not a substitute. Wise allows you to hold a VND balance, spend via the Wise debit card, and withdraw from ATMs in Vietnam without excessive FX fees. However, Wise does not provide a Vietnamese local account number, so you cannot receive local salary transfers, receive payments from Vietnamese banks, or use it for landlord-to-tenant domestic transfers. Note: Wise cards are issued based on your country of registration — if you registered Wise in Vietnam, the card may not be available. If registered elsewhere, it will work in Vietnam.

**Can I use Apple Pay or Google Pay in Vietnam?**

Yes. In 2026, Techcombank, Vietcombank, and VPBank all support Apple Pay and Google Pay. You will need a Visa or Mastercard debit card to link to these services — a NAPAS-only card will not work.

**How do I send money out of Vietnam?**

Outbound international remittance requires you to demonstrate that funds were legally earned and taxed in Vietnam. Banks typically ask for your labor contract, payroll statements, and a Personal Income Tax (PIT) clearance certificate from the General Department of Taxation. Specific document requirements and transfer limits vary by bank and amount. Confirm the current requirements directly with your branch before attempting a large transfer.

**What happens when I renew my visa?**

Bring your new TRC to the branch immediately after renewal and update your account records. Do not wait. Some banks require the update within 30 days to keep the account fully active.

## Summary

Banking in Vietnam in 2026 is not difficult if you have the right visa status and documents. If you hold a TRC, choose Techcombank for the app or Vietcombank for the ATM network, complete biometric registration at the branch on day one, request a Visa or Mastercard debit, activate Smart OTP, and manage your account around visa renewal dates.

If you are on a tourist visa, use your home-country card for ATM withdrawals and [SafetyWing](https://safetywing.com/?referenceID=26505542&utm_source=26505542&utm_medium=Ambassador) or Wise for spending. Opening a local account is not worth pursuing until your residency status changes.
