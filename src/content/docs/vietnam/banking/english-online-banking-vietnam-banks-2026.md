For foreigners in Vietnam, the safest question is not “which bank app has English?” It is:

> Can a non-Vietnamese-speaker open the account, activate the app, update biometric information, transfer money, recover access, and get support without relying on Vietnamese help?

On that standard, the answer is mixed.

The most cautious ranking is:

1. **HSBC Vietnam** — strongest documented English-language banking ecosystem, but foreigners still need to meet eligibility requirements and may need branch or transaction-office setup.
2. **BIDV** — strongest domestic-bank evidence for published foreigner e-banking eligibility, but current 2026 branch conditions should be checked.
3. **Techcombank** — polished domestic app candidate, but foreigner self-service and recovery should not be assumed.
4. **Vietcombank** — strong mainstream bank, but foreigner biometric updates can require a transaction-point visit.
5. **VPBank** — app English is visible, but public foreigner-specific setup and recovery evidence is thinner.

This is not a statement that HSBC is the best bank for every foreigner, or that domestic banks are unusable. It means the evidence is strongest for HSBC on English-language support, while domestic banks may still be better for local salary, QR payments, domestic transfers, branch network, or employer-assisted setup.

## What “usable English online banking” really means

A bank app can show English in the app store and still be hard to use in real life.

For foreigners in Vietnam, usable English banking has six layers:

| Layer | What to check | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| App language | Is English available in the app interface? | Useful, but not enough by itself |
| Account setup | Can a foreigner open or activate the account? | Some online flows are built around Vietnamese citizen ID |
| Biometric update | Can the user update biometric data without a Vietnamese ID card? | 2026 banking access can depend on identity and biometric status |
| Transfer flow | Are local transfers, QR payments, and limits usable? | Daily life often depends on local transfers, not only card spending |
| Support | Can the bank help in English by phone, chat, message, or branch? | Support channels do not always mean English-speaking help |
| Recovery | What happens after a new phone, SIM change, passport change, visa/TRC renewal, or locked app? | Recovery is often the real test of bank usability |

This is why the article separates three things:

- **English listed** — the app or website shows English as a supported language.
- **English usable** — normal daily flows can be managed without Vietnamese help.
- **English recoverable** — account access can be restored when something goes wrong.

For non-Vietnamese-speakers, “recoverable” is often more important than a polished app menu.

## The 2026 banking layer: biometric and document checks matter

Vietnam’s payment-account, online-banking, and bank-card rules have become more identity- and security-sensitive since 2024 and 2025.

The practical takeaway for foreigners is not that every transaction has the same biometric rule. It is more specific:

- banks may require stronger authentication for some online and card transactions;
- banks may need current customer identity information;
- foreign customers may need passport, visa, residence-period, or equivalent documentation;
- biometric or document mismatch problems may need branch support;
- a bank that works normally today may become difficult after a phone, passport, visa/TRC, or device change.

Circular No. 17/2024/TT-NHNN regulating the opening and use of payment accounts at payment service providers, effective from 1 July 2024 with staged provisions from 1 October 2024, 1 January 2025, 1 July 2025, and 1 January 2026, governs payment accounts. Decision No. 2345/QD-NHNN dated 18 December 2023 on application of safety and security measures to online payment and card payment, effective from 1 July 2024, establishes online payment and card-payment security requirements. Circular No. 18/2024/TT-NHNN regulating bank card operations, effective from 1 July 2024 with selected provisions effective from 1 October 2024, 1 January 2025, and 1 July 2025, governs bank cards. Circular No. 50/2024/TT-NHNN providing for security and confidentiality during provision of online banking services, effective from 1 January 2025 with staged provisions through 1 July 2026, governs online-banking security. Circular No. 45/2025/TT-NHNN on amendments to certain Articles of Circular No. 18/2024/TT-NHNN on bank card operations, effective from 5 January 2026, amends the bank-card operations framework. These rules should not be collapsed into a single claim such as “biometrics are required for all online banking.”

The practical layer is this: for foreigners, biometric and document updates are not just compliance details. They affect whether the banking app keeps working.

This is especially important for people who:

- change passport;
- renew a visa or TRC;
- change Vietnamese SIM card;
- replace a phone;
- lose app access overseas;
- need high-value transfers;
- rely on the app for daily QR payments or domestic transfers.

For ATM and card-specific planning, see [ATMs in Vietnam for expats](/vietnam/banking/atms-vietnam-expats-2026).

## Bank-by-bank comparison

## HSBC Vietnam

HSBC Vietnam has the strongest documented English-language ecosystem among the banks checked.

The official layer is relatively clear. HSBC publishes extensive English materials for accounts, app use, online banking, support channels, foreigner documentation, and biometric update. Foreign residents can be eligible for account products if they meet conditions such as age, Vietnam residence, and valid visa, visa-equivalent, visa-exemption, passport, or equivalent documentation depending on product and case.

The practical layer is more conditional. HSBC’s online account opening is not a general foreigner self-service route. HSBC’s own account-opening materials direct foreign residents toward branch or transaction-office support rather than pure online opening.

That makes HSBC a strong candidate for non-Vietnamese-speakers who value English documentation and support. It does not make HSBC the automatic best bank for every foreigner.

HSBC is most realistic for:

- long-stay foreign residents who can meet account documentation requirements;
- people who value English-language banking support more than the largest domestic branch network;
- users who may need international banking features;
- people who are comfortable with branch or transaction-office setup;
- users who want a bank with clearer English guidance on app, online banking, support, and biometric update.

HSBC may be less ideal for:

- short-stay visitors expecting a quick local account;
- users who mainly need the cheapest possible daily banking setup;
- people who need a domestic-bank-heavy workflow for salary, QR payments, local merchant transfers, or employer banking;
- anyone assuming the entire process can be completed online as a foreigner.

Safe conclusion:

> HSBC Vietnam is the best documented English-support candidate among the banks checked, but it remains conditional on account eligibility, branch or transaction-office setup, biometric update, and product fit.

## BIDV

BIDV is one of the stronger domestic-bank candidates on paper.

The official layer is useful: BIDV has published information indicating that foreigners and Vietnamese non-residents can register for BIDV SmartBanking and BIDV Online if account, age, capacity, and phone-number conditions are met. BIDV SmartBanking is also listed with English language support in app-store metadata. BIDV publishes support channels, including hotline and an English IVR path.

The practical layer needs caution. The foreigner e-banking source is older, and public information does not prove that every 2026 branch, account type, or recovery case will be easy for a non-Vietnamese-speaker. An English IVR option is useful evidence, but it is not the same as guaranteed English-speaking support for account recovery, biometric problems, document mismatch, or branch-level exceptions.

BIDV is most realistic for:

- foreigners with a local account and Vietnamese phone number;
- long-stay residents whose employer or branch can help with setup;
- users who want a major domestic bank with published foreigner e-banking eligibility;
- people who need local payment rails more than premium international support.

BIDV is weaker for:

- users who expect a fully English self-service journey;
- foreigners with no Vietnamese-language backup during setup;
- short-stay users who are not sure they qualify for a local account;
- people who need highly predictable English recovery support.

Safe conclusion:

> BIDV looks like one of the stronger domestic-bank candidates for foreigners on paper, but a non-Vietnamese-speaker should confirm current branch setup, account conditions, and recovery support before relying on it.

## Vietcombank

Vietcombank is a major domestic-bank option, but not the cleanest English-only choice.

The platform layer is positive: VCB Digibank is listed with English language support in app-store metadata.

The official-practical split is important. Vietcombank has stated that foreign customers cannot use the same chip-based citizen ID and National Population Database biometric paths as Vietnamese customers. Foreign customers may need to visit a Vietcombank transaction point with passport and documents verifying valid residence period.

That does not mean Vietcombank is unusable. It means the user should not judge Vietcombank only by app language.

Vietcombank is most realistic for:

- foreigners who need a mainstream domestic bank;
- people with employer or Vietnamese-speaking setup support;
- users who can visit a branch or transaction point when biometric or document issues appear;
- people who value domestic network strength over English-first service.

Vietcombank is weaker for:

- non-Vietnamese-speakers expecting app-only setup and recovery;
- users who cannot easily visit a transaction point;
- people likely to change passport, visa/TRC, phone, or device without branch support.

Safe conclusion:

> Vietcombank may work well after setup, but foreigners should expect biometric and document-update steps to be more branch-dependent than the app interface suggests.

## Techcombank

Techcombank is a plausible domestic-bank app candidate, but foreigner self-service should not be assumed.

The platform layer is positive: Techcombank Mobile is listed with English language support in app-store metadata, and the bank has a modern digital-banking profile.

The practical layer is more cautious. Techcombank’s app-based opening materials refer to chip-based ID scanning, which does not automatically prove a foreign-passport opening path. Techcombank also has foreigner-facing biometric support language that points foreign customers toward bringing a passport to a branch for help.

This makes Techcombank a reasonable candidate to compare, but not a confirmed “all online, all English” answer for foreigners.

Techcombank is most realistic for:

- long-stay users who can confirm branch setup first;
- people who value a modern domestic-bank app;
- users with local support during onboarding;
- foreigners who mainly need domestic transfers and app-based daily banking after setup.

Techcombank is weaker for:

- users who need proof of fully online foreigner account opening;
- people with no Vietnamese-speaking fallback;
- users who cannot easily handle branch biometric or document checks.

Safe conclusion:

> Techcombank can be a strong domestic app candidate, but the safer assumption is that foreigners may still need branch support for biometric, document, or recovery steps.

## VPBank

VPBank should be included in the comparison, but public foreigner-specific evidence is thinner.

The platform layer is positive: VPBank NEO is listed with English language support in app-store metadata, and VPBank publishes digital-banking and support-channel materials.

The practical layer is less certain. Public English evidence does not clearly prove that a foreign-passport user can complete full online opening, biometric update, and account recovery through VPBank NEO without Vietnamese help.

That does not mean VPBank cannot work. It means the article should not over-rank it for non-Vietnamese-speakers based only on app metadata.

VPBank is most realistic for:

- users who can verify branch conditions before opening;
- foreigners with employer or Vietnamese-speaking support;
- people already leaning toward VPBank for employer, salary, card, or branch reasons;
- users comfortable treating the app as a tool after setup, not the whole banking journey.

VPBank is weaker for:

- non-Vietnamese-speakers choosing a bank only from public English evidence;
- users who need strong proof of foreigner online opening;
- people who need predictable English recovery support.

Safe conclusion:

> VPBank NEO may be usable after setup, but it is not the strongest documented first choice for non-Vietnamese-speakers based on public foreigner-specific evidence.

Choose **HSBC Vietnam first to investigate**.

Start with **BIDV, Vietcombank, or Techcombank**, depending on employer support, branch access, and setup help.

A domestic bank may be more convenient for:

- salary receipt from a Vietnamese employer;
- VietQR-heavy daily payments;
- domestic bank transfers;
- local ATM access;
- merchant compatibility;
- building management or landlord transfer requests.

For domestic transfers and VietQR reality, see [sending money within Vietnam](/vietnam/banking/send-money-within-vietnam-2026).

## If the user has no Vietnamese-speaking backup

Start with **HSBC Vietnam**, then compare **BIDV** only if the local branch can confirm foreigner setup and support.

The weaker setup is choosing a domestic bank only because the app store lists English.

The better setup is asking before opening:

- Can foreign customers open this account with passport and current visa/TRC documents?
- Can the app be activated in English?
- What happens if biometric update fails?
- Can support handle recovery in English?
- Does a passport change require branch update?
- Can the bank support a new phone or SIM change?
- Are domestic transfers and QR payments available for this account type?

## If the user already has employer support

A domestic bank may be more realistic.

Many foreign employees do not choose a bank only by app quality. Payroll setup, HR support, branch familiarity, and employer banking relationships often matter. In that case, the most useful question is not “which bank is best?” but:

> Which bank will the employer help set up, and can the employee recover access later without Vietnamese help?

For long-stay employees, BIDV, Vietcombank, Techcombank, or VPBank may be workable if the employer helps with onboarding and the branch confirms app and biometric support.

## If the user is short-stay or not sure about local account eligibility

Do not assume a Vietnamese bank account will be available or worth the effort.

A short-stay visitor, tourist-status user, or digital nomad without stable Vietnam documentation may need a backup setup first:

- foreign Visa or Mastercard;
- some cash;
- an ATM plan;
- a local SIM for OTP and app use;
- a multi-currency card for backup spending or ATM access;
- a local bank account only if documentation and stay length justify it.

## Common mistakes

## Mistake 1: choosing by app-store language only

English in the app listing is useful, but it is not enough.

The app may still be difficult when the user needs biometric update, branch verification, transfer confirmation, complaint handling, or recovery after a device change.

## Mistake 2: assuming online opening applies to foreign passports

Some banks advertise fast online opening, but those flows may depend on Vietnamese citizen ID, VNeID, or chip-based ID infrastructure.

For foreign-passport users, branch or transaction-office setup may still be the practical route.

## Mistake 3: treating HSBC as automatically best

HSBC has the strongest documented English ecosystem among the banks checked. But it may not be the best fit for everyone.

A domestic bank may be better for salary, local transfers, QR payments, ATM access, or employer-supported setup.

## Mistake 4: treating domestic banks as unusable

Domestic banks can be realistic choices, especially after setup. The risk is not that they never work. The risk is that a non-Vietnamese-speaker may struggle when something breaks.

## Mistake 5: ignoring passport, visa, TRC, phone, and device changes

A bank account can work for months, then become difficult after:

- passport renewal;
- visa or TRC renewal;
- phone replacement;
- SIM change;
- app reinstall;
- biometric update request;
- high-value transaction;
- overseas login or travel.

These are the moments when English recovery support matters.

## FAQ

## Can foreigners use Vietnamese banking apps in English?

Often, yes at the interface level, but not always at the full-service level. Several major bank apps are listed with English and Vietnamese. That does not prove that account opening, biometric update, transfer recovery, complaints, or branch support will all work smoothly in English.

## Which Vietnamese bank is best for non-Vietnamese-speakers?

HSBC Vietnam has the strongest documented English-language ecosystem among the banks checked. That does not make it the best bank for every foreigner. For domestic salary, QR payments, local transfers, and employer support, a domestic bank may still fit better.

## Is BIDV good for foreigners?

BIDV has useful public evidence for foreigner and non-resident e-banking eligibility, plus app English metadata and an English IVR path. The caution is that the foreigner e-banking source is older, so current branch and account conditions should be confirmed before relying on it.

## Is Vietcombank good for foreigners?

Vietcombank can be a practical domestic-bank option, especially for people who need a major local bank. But foreign customers may need to visit a transaction point for biometric update because Vietnamese citizen ID infrastructure does not apply to them. It should not be treated as an English-only self-service option.

## Is Techcombank good for foreigners?

Techcombank is a plausible domestic app candidate, and its app is listed with English. But public materials do not prove that foreign-passport users can complete every setup and recovery step online. Foreigners should confirm branch support, biometric update, and document requirements first.

## Is VPBank good for foreigners?

VPBank NEO is listed with English, and VPBank publishes digital-banking materials. However, public foreigner-specific evidence for online opening and recovery is weaker than for some other banks in this comparison. It is safer to treat VPBank as branch-confirmation required.

## Can foreigners open Vietnamese bank accounts fully online?

Do not assume that. Some bank marketing promotes online or app-based opening, but those flows may rely on Vietnamese citizen ID infrastructure. HSBC Vietnam, for example, directs foreign residents toward branch or transaction-office routes rather than pure online opening.

## What matters more: English app or English support?

English support matters more. A banking app can be usable for daily checking and transfers, but account recovery, biometric update, passport update, phone change, or a blocked transaction can require support. Non-Vietnamese-speakers should choose based on recovery path, not only app menus.

## Should a foreigner still keep a foreign card?

Yes. A foreign card or multi-currency card is a useful backup while local banking setup, biometric update, or account recovery is unresolved. It should not be treated as a full replacement for a Vietnamese bank account if the user needs salary receipt, domestic transfers, or VietQR-heavy daily payments.
