Da Nang is the Vietnamese city that foreigners don't visit — they move to. While Hanoi and Saigon are places nomads endure for 3 months before burning out, Da Nang is where they sign 12-month leases and start Googling long-term visa options. It operates on a completely different frequency: the air is breathable, the beach is the geographic anchor, and the cost of living is noticeably lower than the two mega-cities.

However, navigating the housing market here in 2026 requires understanding massive recent shifts. In July 2025, Da Nang officially absorbed the neighboring Quang Nam province, tripling its size and swallowing Hoi An. The government completely abolished the traditional "District" (*quận*) system, replacing it with massive, consolidated wards. On top of that, unlike Ho Chi Minh City, Da Nang has zero metro lines and no plans to build one. If you move here, you live on a motorbike. Here is the unfiltered reality of where to base yourself for a long-term stay.

## Key Takeaways

- **The price floor.** Renting equivalent square footage in Da Nang is roughly 30–40% cheaper than in Hanoi or HCMC.
- **The Hoi An reality.** Legally, Hoi An is no longer an independent city. Following the July 2025 reform, it was downgraded into three specific wards within Da Nang municipality.
- **The typhoon trap.** Landlords rarely volunteer information about flood zones. Signing a lease in September without checking the street's drainage history is a guaranteed way to lose your deposit and your electronics.
- **No metro access.** Da Nang has no subway, no BRT, and no realistic rail plans for the next decade. You rely entirely on Grab or a rented scooter.
- **Fierce seasonality.** Unlike Hanoi or HCMC, Da Nang is a domestic tourism city. Short-term rents spike 40–50% between June and August when Vietnamese families flood the beach — don't try to land a 1-month lease in July.

## 1. An Thuong (The Expat Core)

### The Vibe
This is the Canggu or Chiang Mai of Central Vietnam. A dense, highly walkable grid of streets just south of the Dragon Bridge, entirely built around western conveniences, remote work, and surf culture.

### Who Lives Here
Long-term lifestyle nomads, semi-retired expats on extended visas, foreign English teachers, and a noticeable Russian long-stay community escaping cold winters.

### Rent Ranges
- **Studio / 1BR:** $250 – $450 / month
- **2BR Apartment:** $450 – $700 / month
- **Independent Tube House (3–4 floors):** $750 – $1,200 / month

*Source: Facebook "Da Nang Expats" listings, TDT Realty Da Nang — April 2026.*

### Daily Life
- **Co-working:** Beans Workspace, Enouvo Space (popular with software developers and remote product teams).
- **Groceries:** K-Mart, L's Place for imported cheese and wine. For bulk groceries, residents rely on delivery from the massive Lotte Mart.
- **Dining:** Dense concentration of vegan bowls, craft burgers, and IPAs — Roots Plant-based, Heart of Darkness. Authentic cheap local food is harder to find without a foreigner tax.
- **Fitness/sports:** My Khe Fitness, plus dozens of surfboard rental racks and SUP clubs right on the sand.
- **Medical:** Family Hospital Da Nang — private, fast, excellent English.

### Getting Around
- **Transport:** You walk to the beach and ride a motorbike everywhere else. Grab cars and bikes swarm the area 24/7.
- **Distances:** 15 minutes to Da Nang International Airport, 40 minutes south to Hoi An.

### The Honest Downsides
- **The flood zone.** An Thuong is notorious for heavy flooding from September to December. Ground-floor apartments frequently take on water.
- It is an intense expat bubble. You rarely interact with locals outside of the service industry.
- During the summer months, the nearby beach access points become uncomfortably packed with tour buses.

### Best For
- **Long-term lifestyle nomad / semi-retired:** Highly recommended. The easiest place to land and build a routine.
- **Short-term nomad:** Highly recommended.
- **Families:** Neutral. The streets are loud and space is cramped unless you rent a full tube house.

## 2. My An / My Khe Beach (The High-Rise Strip)

### The Vibe
Stretching north along the coast toward the Son Tra mountain base, this strip is defined by towering sea-view condo buildings, massive seafood restaurants, and heavy tourist traffic.

### Who Lives Here
Short-term nomads testing out the city, Korean retirees, and transient holiday crowds.

### Rent Ranges
- **Studio / 1BR:** $300 – $550 / month (premium paid for unobstructed ocean views)
- **2BR Apartment:** $500 – $900 / month (e.g., Muong Thanh and newer condo projects)
- **Independent Tube House:** Extremely rare here; the zoning is dominated by high-rises.

*Source: Rentapartment.vn Da Nang, batdongsan.com.vn — April 2026.*

### Daily Life
- **Co-working:** The Hub Da Nang coastal branch.
- **Dining:** This is ground zero for Da Nang's massive open-air seafood restaurants and Korean BBQ joints. Western cafes are sparser than in An Thuong.
- **Medical:** Hoan My Hospital is within 10 minutes by Grab.

### Getting Around
- **Transport:** The main coastal road (Vo Nguyen Giap) is wide and easy on a scooter, though you share it with aggressive 45-seater tour buses.
- **Distances:** 10 minutes to the airport, 35 minutes south to Hoi An.

### The Honest Downsides
- Noise from the highway and the constant flow of tour groups can be grating on lower floors.
- Certain high-rises, like the older Muong Thanh buildings, have a history of complex ownership disputes and severe fire safety compliance issues.
- Coastal erosion has visibly chewed away sections of the beach directly across from the main highway in 2025 and 2026.

### Best For
- **Short-term nomad:** Highly recommended. Easy turnkey apartments with ocean views.
- **Long-term lifestyle nomad:** Recommended if you refuse to live without a 20th-floor ocean view.
- **Families:** Neutral.

## 3. Coastal Villa Compounds (Ocean Villas & The Point)

### The Vibe
Located far south along the coastal highway toward Hoi An, these are gated, heavily secured enclaves built around golf courses and private beaches. Quiet, manicured, and completely isolated from the city's street life.

### Who Lives Here
Wealthy semi-retired expats, foreign executives, and western families prioritizing space over urban convenience.

### Rent Ranges
- **Studio / 1BR:** N/A — these are exclusively villa communities.
- **3–4BR Luxury Villa (with private pool):** $1,500 – $3,500+ / month

*Source: Tony Real Estate Da Nang, Ocean Villas internal leasing — April 2026.*

### Daily Life
- **Amenities:** Life happens inside the compound. You rely on the golf club restaurants, five-star hotel dining, or getting groceries delivered.
- **Schools:** St. Nicholas International School and APU American International School are situated along this southern corridor.
- **Medical:** Family Medical Practice (An Phu branch) is the closest international clinic, roughly 20 minutes north.

### Getting Around
- **Transport:** You must own a car or a powerful scooter. You cannot walk anywhere useful.
- **Distances:** A solid 20-minute drive north into Da Nang city, or 20 minutes south to Hoi An.

### The Honest Downsides
- Extreme isolation. Getting a coffee or buying basic vegetables requires a commute.
- Grab drivers frequently cancel requests from these compounds late at night because they do not want to drive the empty coastal highway back to the city.
- Services catering to daily life are sparse — you pay for the privacy.

### Best For
- **Semi-retired (1+ years):** Highly recommended. Total peace and high security.
- **Families:** Highly recommended. Safe roads for kids and proximity to international schools.
- **Long-term / short-term nomad:** Not recommended. You will go stir-crazy.

## 4. Hai Chau Downtown (The CBD)

### The Vibe
Located on the west bank of the Han River, this is the historic and commercial core of actual Da Nang. Heavy tree canopies, deep local alleyways, government buildings, and a thriving Vietnamese cafe culture.

### Who Lives Here
Long-term expats on tight budgets, foreign English teachers, and foreigners who specifically want to integrate into Vietnamese society.

### Rent Ranges
- **1BR / Serviced Room:** $200 – $350 / month
- **2BR Apartment:** $400 – $600 / month
- **Independent Tube House:** $500 – $800 / month

*Source: ChoTot, local Vietnamese Facebook housing groups — April 2026.*

### Daily Life
- **Cafes:** Cong Caphe, plus hundreds of high-quality independent Vietnamese coffee shops. They rarely market themselves as co-working spaces, but they are excellent for laptop work.
- **Groceries:** Vincom Plaza (modern supermarket) and Con Market (an intense, authentic traditional wet market).
- **Medical:** Vinmec Da Nang International Hospital.

### Getting Around
- **Transport:** Grab is everywhere. Riding a motorbike here requires more skill than in An Thuong due to tighter traffic and one-way streets.
- **Distances:** 5 to 10 minutes to the airport, 15 minutes over the bridge to the beach.

### The Honest Downsides
- You cannot walk to the ocean.
- During peak summer months (April to August), the concrete density makes Hai Chau feel significantly hotter and more stifling than the breezy coastal side.
- English proficiency among street vendors and local landlords drops off a cliff compared to An Thuong.

### Best For
- **Long-term lifestyle nomad:** Recommended for those optimizing for deep value and cultural immersion.
- **Short-term nomad:** Not recommended.
- **Families:** Neutral.

## 5. The Brief Alternatives

### Non Nuoc / Marble Mountain
- **The vibe:** A sleepy stretch of southern beach dominated by the towering Marble Mountains and a heavy Korean retirement community.
- **Rent:** $200–$300 for a 1BR. Noticeably cheaper than An Thuong.
- **Best for:** Introverted nomads and semi-retirees on a strict budget.
- **Trade-off:** The beach is pristine and empty. But western amenities are scarce, and driving into the city for social events takes 15 minutes.

### The Summit / Da Phuoc
- **The vibe:** A massive reclaimed land project at the northern mouth of the Han River. Wide, empty boulevards and half-finished high-rises.
- **Rent:** $200–$300 for a brand new 1BR.
- **Best for:** People who want brand-new appliances and do not care about neighborhood atmosphere.
- **Trade-off:** The air is incredibly fresh and the apartments are modern. But it suffers from a severe ghost-town effect — vacancy rates in the mega-blocks are high and the streets feel deserted at night.

### Hoa Xuan / FPT City
- **The vibe:** A deep inland suburban development wrapped by rivers, featuring massive empty plots and new standalone houses built by the local middle class.
- **Rent:** $150–$250 for an apartment, or $300–$400 for a massive multi-story house.
- **Best for:** Hermit creatives who need a massive house for cheap.
- **Trade-off:** The infrastructure is flawless and it is dead silent. But there are practically zero foreigners, and it takes 25 minutes of driving to reach the beach or the city center.

## 6. Da Nang vs Hoi An — Which Should You Pick?

With the July 2025 merger, Hoi An is technically just a cluster of wards inside Da Nang. But practically, culturally, and financially, choosing between the two dictates your entire lifestyle.

**The administrative reality.**
Hoi An is no longer a city. It was downgraded into three specific wards under Da Nang's jurisdiction: Hoi An Ward (the ancient town core), Hoi An Dong Ward (Cua Dai Beach area), and Hoi An Tay Ward (An Bang Beach and Cam An). Locals and expats still treat it as a distinct world. The two hubs are separated by about 30 kilometers — a 40 to 50-minute motorbike ride down the coastal highway, or a $10 Grab car ride.

**The crowd and lifestyle.**
Da Nang attracts tech workers, e-commerce sellers, and efficiency-focused nomads. You live there for the co-working spaces, international hospitals, and fast internet. Hoi An attracts yoga instructors, artists, and slow-travel retirees. You live there to ride a bicycle through rice paddies, drink coffee in centuries-old merchant houses, and accept a much slower pace of life.

**The rent reversal.**
If you rent a basic house deep in the Hoi An rice paddies, it is incredibly cheap — $350 to $500 per month. But the western expat community in Hoi An is fiercely concentrated around **An Bang Beach**. Because supply there is so limited and demand from wealthy westerners is so high, renting a house with a yard in An Bang now costs **$800 to $1,500 per month**. It is ironically more expensive than living in Da Nang's An Thuong expat core.

**The verdict.**
If you need high-speed infrastructure, diverse dining, and proximity to an airport, choose Da Nang. If you want extreme quiet, nature, and do not mind traveling 45 minutes for a western medical clinic, choose Hoi An. Many long-term expats do both — they rent cheap in Da Nang and take weekend trips to Hoi An.

## 7. Five Key Features of the 2026 Da Nang Rental Market

Da Nang's housing market operates on completely different rules than the capital cities.

**1. The absolute lack of transit.** Da Nang has no metro, no BRT, and no plans to build one. You either learn to ride a motorbike, or you bleed money relying on Grab cars every single day.

**2. The typhoon trap.** From September to December, Da Nang gets battered by heavy monsoons and typhoons. Many landlords use standard contracts that lack a force majeure or weather damage escape clause. Countless nomads have rented a cheap ground-floor tube house in July, watched it flood with half a meter of water in October, and lost their 2-month deposit trying to break the lease early. Always rent on the second floor or higher if you arrive before October.

**3. The tube house electricity pit.** Renting a 4-story tube house for $600 a month sounds like a steal until summer hits. Because these tall, narrow concrete houses bleed cold air, running air conditioning across multiple poorly-insulated floors can result in monthly electricity bills hitting $150–$200.

**4. Extreme seasonality.** Da Nang is the domestic vacation capital of Vietnam. Between June and August, millions of local tourists flood the city. Finding a short-term, 1-month rental during this window is nearly impossible without paying double the standard rate. Long-term (6–12 month) leases are insulated from this, but do not try to negotiate a short stint in July.

**5. Compliance premiums.** Just like the mega-cities, Da Nang landlords were hit by the Housing Law 2023 (effective January 2025) and the Law on Fire Prevention, Firefighting and Rescue 2024 (effective July 2025). Cheap, non-compliant studio buildings hidden deep in alleys were shut down, pushing the baseline rent for legal, fire-compliant 1BR apartments up across the city. For realistic long-term hunting, bypass Airbnb and post your requirements directly in the *Da Nang Expats* and *Expats in Da Nang* Facebook groups. Watch out for the same Hold Deposit Scams and bait-and-switch tactics that plague Hanoi and Saigon — our [guide to avoiding rental scams in Vietnam](/vietnam/housing/avoid-rental-scams-vietnam-2026) covers the patterns in detail.

## 9. How to Choose: A Quick Decision Framework

**1. How long are you staying?**
If you are testing the waters for 1 to 3 months, pay the premium for a serviced apartment in An Thuong or My An. Skip the hassle of utility bills and buying a motorbike immediately. Since most short-term nomads rent scooters here — and scooter accidents are the single most common medical claim filed by foreigners in Vietnam — a travel medical plan is worth having before you arrive. [SafetyWing's Nomad Insurance](https://safetywing.com/?referenceID=26505542&utm_source=26505542&utm_medium=Ambassador) runs about $56.28 per 4 weeks for travelers aged 10–39 and covers scooter accidents (unlike many standard travel policies), plus weekend trips to Hoi An, Hue, or Bangkok. Family Hospital Da Nang accepts international insurance claims directly. If you are staying 6+ months, rent a house in Hai Chau or a 2BR deep in An Thuong to cut costs.

**2. What is your budget?**
If you have $800+ for rent, you can afford a high-rise ocean view in My An or a great house in An Thuong. If your hard ceiling is $300, you need to look west of the river in Hai Chau or south toward Non Nuoc.

**3. Will you ride a motorbike?**
This is the question that actually determines your neighborhood. If you refuse to drive a scooter, do not live in the Coastal Villa Compounds, Non Nuoc, or Hoa Xuan — you will be trapped. You must stay inside the highly walkable An Thuong grid, or commit to an ocean-view high-rise in My An where Grab is always 2 minutes away.

## 10. The 2025 Administrative Reform: What Expats Need to Know

On July 1, 2025, Da Nang executed a historic merger, absorbing Quang Nam province. The city ballooned to roughly 11,860 square kilometers with a population of roughly 2.8 million — and inherited two UNESCO World Heritage sites (Hoi An Ancient Town and My Son Sanctuary) in the process.

In practice, the government abolished the "District" (*quận*) tier in the urban core. For Grab drivers, Google Maps, and daily expat conversations, the old names (Hai Chau, An Thuong, Hoi An) are still universally used. However, when you open a local bank account (see our [Vietnam bank account guide](/vietnam/banking/bank-account-guide-2026)) or register your address for a visa extension (covered in our [Vietnam visa guide](/vietnam/visa/visa-guide-2026)), your lease agreement **must** reflect the new official wards (*phường*).

**The 2026 ward mapping cheat sheet:**

- **An Thuong + Coastal Villa Compounds (Ocean Villas / The Point):** Both now inside **Ngu Hanh Son Ward** — a consolidation of the former My An, Khue My, Hoa Hai, and Hoa Quy wards. Yes, the expat core and the luxury golf villas are now legally the same ward.
- **My Khe Beach (North) / My An:** Now merged into **An Hai Ward** (former Phuoc My, An Hai Bac, An Hai Nam).
- **Hai Chau Downtown (CBD):** Now merged into **Hai Chau Ward** (former Thanh Binh, Thuan Phuoc, Thach Thang, Phuoc Ninh, old Hai Chau).
- **Son Tra Peninsula (near the mountain):** Now merged into **Son Tra Ward** (former Tho Quang, Nai Hien Dong, Man Thai).
- **Hoi An Ancient Town core:** Now **Hoi An Ward** (former Minh An, Cam Pho, Son Phong, Cam Nam + Cam Kim commune).
- **An Bang Beach + Cam An:** Now **Hoi An Tay Ward** (former Thanh Ha, Tan An, Cam An + Cam Ha commune).
- **Cua Dai Beach + Cam Thanh:** Now **Hoi An Dong Ward** (former Cam Chau, Cua Dai + Cam Thanh commune).

Always make sure your landlord writes the exact, legally updated 2026 ward name on your contract. A mismatch between your lease address and the police registration system will cause problems when you extend a visa or open a bank account.
