## The short answer

Da Nang's clearest remote-work cafe cluster is still around My An and the north edge of An Thuong, where SakeCafé & Workspace, Lu Coffee, and nearby late-hours options give the most choice within walking distance. Hômnay is the clearer in-zone An Thuong pick if food matters and the plan is more than a quick drink. A-kopi works better as a Han River meeting point than as a full-day deep-work base. Danasol is one of the clearer east-of-river options in An Hai / Sơn Trà. For scheduled calls, client meetings, or a full workday, a coworking pass is often the better answer than stretching a cafe session too far.

*Source note: locations, public hours, menu bands, and WiFi references below are based on current public listings and recent work-cafe platforms checked in April 2026. Treat hours, seating conditions, and speed-test numbers as approximate.*

## How to use this shortlist

The hard part is not finding coffee in Da Nang. The hard part is finding a cafe that still works once the laptop is open for two to four hours.

This shortlist gives priority to six things:

- neighborhood fit
- realistic WiFi, not marketing language alone
- power access in practice
- noise level by likely use case
- whether a long sit feels normal or awkward
- rough spend, so the workday cost is clear before ordering

If apartment internet is weak, keep a local data backup ready. Our [Vietnam Essential Apps](/vietnam/daily-life/vietnam-essential-apps-2026) guide and [Home Internet in Vietnam](/vietnam/daily-life/home-internet-vietnam-expats-2026) guide help with that part of the setup.

## My An: the best starting point for most remote workers

For most readers staying near My Khe, My An is the easiest place to start. The cafe density is better, the walk between options is short, and the work-friendly choices are stronger than they are in most other Da Nang zones.

### SakeCafé & Workspace

Sake is one of the clearest default picks for focused solo work near Nguyễn Văn Thoại. Recent work-cafe platforms show strong speed tests and socket-friendly setups, which makes it a good first try for readers who want a proper laptop session rather than a photo stop.

Use it for:
- focused solo work
- a two-to-four-hour block near the beach
- mornings when the plan is to get work done before going anywhere else

Price range:
- expect a specialty-cafe band rather than cheap street-coffee pricing
- roughly 35,000-60,000 VND for drinks is a workable planning range in this part of the market
- food or repeat orders will push the total higher

Reality check:
Public work-cafe platforms are a useful signal here, but they do not guarantee the same experience at every table or every hour.

### Lu Coffee

Lu Coffee is one of the easier My An picks for a one-to-three-hour session. Public menu pricing is visible, the location is simple to reach from the beach-side apartment zone, and the drink spend is easier to estimate before arriving.

Use it for:
- a shorter focused block
- a quieter coffee-first stop
- readers who care about drink quality and do not need a meal-heavy setup

Price range:
- current public menu items start around 35,000 VND
- many signature drinks and desserts sit around 39,000-49,000 VND
- a light coffee-and-pastry stop usually stays reasonable without turning into coworking-level spend

Reality check:
Lu is a better short-to-medium session choice than a guaranteed all-day base. If the day includes multiple calls or a six-hour block, coworking is safer.

### HI4 Coffee & Workspace

HI4 is the late-hours outlier in this zone. If the schedule starts early, runs late, or the normal cafe window is already gone, this is one of the few names worth checking first.

Use it for:
- late-night work
- early starts
- students and freelancers who want a study-style setup more than a specialty-coffee atmosphere

Price range:
- current public menu bands show many drinks around 25,000-45,000 VND
- that makes HI4 easier to use for longer sits than some brunch-heavy cafes

Reality check:
HI4 publicly markets itself as 24/7, but late-night quietness, seat choice, and network quality can still vary. Treat the long hours as a current public-facing claim, not a promise that every overnight session will feel the same.

## An Thuong: better for walkability and mixed work-and-food days

An Thuong works well for readers who want cafes, lunch, apartments, and beach access in the same small area. It is less about one perfect work cafe and more about having a comfortable base where work can fit into the rest of the day.

### Hômnay Eatery & Cafe

Hômnay is the clearer in-zone An Thuong option if the plan is not just coffee. It is useful when one stop needs to cover work, food, and a longer sit without moving again.

Use it for:
- a two-to-four-hour mixed work block
- laptop time that turns into lunch
- readers staying deeper inside the An Thuong grid, not right on the Nguyễn Văn Thoại side

Price range:
- a drink often lands around 40,000-60,000 VND
- brunch or fuller food orders push the bill higher
- it is not the cheapest way to work, but it makes more sense than bouncing between coffee and lunch stops

Reality check:
Public pages can confirm the address and long opening window. They cannot confirm that the room will still feel quiet at midday. Hômnay is better framed as a flexible work-and-food option than as a silent focus room.

### What An Thuong readers should know before choosing a cafe

If the stay is in An Thuong but the workday is the main priority, the best answer is often to walk a few minutes toward the My An / Bắc Mỹ Phú side rather than forcing a deeper An Thuong cafe to do full-day duty. That is especially true for heavy upload work, repeated calls, or days that need a backup option within the same block.

## Han River / Hai Chau: better for meetings than for marathon solo sessions

The Han River side is useful when the day already involves city-center errands, meetings, or being closer to the core Hai Chau business area. It is less useful if the goal is to stay inside a dense beach-side work-cafe cluster.

### A-kopi Coffee Roaster

A-kopi is one of the clearer central-city cafe anchors for this article because it gives the Han River side a real option without pretending that Hai Chau has the same laptop-cafe density as My An.

Use it for:
- short meetings
- one-to-three-hour city-center work blocks
- readers who want a central stop before or after appointments

Price range:
- expect a lower central-cafe band, roughly around 25,000-40,000 VND for many drinks
- this is a better “work between other things” stop than an ultra-cheap sit-all-day option

Reality check:
A-kopi makes sense as a Han River anchor. It does not change the wider pattern: if the whole day is built around remote work, the beach-side cluster or coworking usually gives more margin for error.

## An Hai / Sơn Trà: the clearest east-of-river option

Readers living east of the river often do not want to cross back toward My An every time they need a work cafe. That is where Danasol becomes useful.

### Danasol

Danasol is one of the clearest An Hai / Sơn Trà-side names because it publicly presents itself as an eatery, cafe, and workspace rather than as a normal coffee shop that sometimes tolerates laptops.

Use it for:
- east-of-river remote work days
- brunch-plus-laptop sessions
- readers who want a clearer work setup without heading back to the beach strip

Price range:
- a basic drink still fits the normal cafe band
- if the session turns into food plus a longer sit, the spend often gets closer to 100,000 VND and up
- that is still below many coworking day passes, but the gap narrows once the order gets larger

Reality check:
Danasol's own positioning and recent work-cafe references are encouraging, especially for WiFi and work intent. The lived experience can still change by seat, crowd level, and time of day.

## When coworking is the better move

A cafe is not always the right answer.

Switch to coworking when:
- the day includes scheduled calls
- the laptop will be open for six hours or more
- upload stability matters
- a second screen, meeting room, or business setting would help
- the cost of multiple cafe orders is already getting close to a day pass

In Da Nang, day passes currently start around 150,000 VND, and many monthly flexible-desk plans land around 1,800,000-2,000,000 VND. ## Practical notes before leaving the apartment

- Re-check hours on the same day around holidays and special events.
- Bring a phone hotspot even if a cafe has strong recent WiFi reports.
- Do not assume every table has an outlet just because the venue has outlets somewhere.
- Order in line with the length of the stay. A cafe is still a cafe, even when it works well for laptops.
