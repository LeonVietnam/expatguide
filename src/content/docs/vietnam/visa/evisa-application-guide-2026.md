## Quick answer: how Vietnam e-visas work in 2026

A Vietnam e-visa is an electronic visa issued through Vietnam's immigration system. For most short-stay visitors who are outside Vietnam and have a valid passport, it is the main self-application route for entering Vietnam without visiting an embassy or consulate.

The official rule layer is simple: eligible foreigners outside Vietnam can personally apply through Vietnam's official electronic visa system, pay the government fee online, check the result with the application details, and download the approved e-visa before travel.

The practical layer is where mistakes happen. The application is not just a formality. The passport scan, portrait photo, name order, passport number, intended dates, entry gate, exit gate, payment status, and final downloaded visa all need to match the trip. A pending application is not approval, and a paid fee does not mean the e-visa has been granted.

This guide is for the e-visa application itself. For a broader comparison of visa exemption, e-visa, visa runs, and longer-stay routes, use the main Vietnam visa guide: [/vietnam/visa/visa-guide-2026](/vietnam/visa/visa-guide-2026).

Those are different immigration questions. If the goal is long-term residence, start with the TRC guide: [/vietnam/visa/trc-application-guide-2026](/vietnam/visa/trc-application-guide-2026).

## Use the official e-visa system, not a lookalike visa site

Start from Vietnam's official immigration or National Electronic Visa system. This matters because the official immigration portal has shown a domain-transition notice: from 11 November 2024, the Vietnam Electronic Visa Portal operates through new domains, including `thithucdientu.gov.vn` and `evisa.gov.vn`.

That does not mean every site using "Vietnam visa" language is official. Commercial visa agencies, search ads, imitation pages, and old pages can appear in the same search results. Some agencies may be legitimate paid services, but their fees, timelines, refund policies, and claims are not the same as the government e-visa process.

Practical rule: reach the e-visa system from an official immigration or government page, check the domain carefully, and avoid paying through an ad or third-party page unless the choice to use a paid agent is deliberate.

## Step 1: prepare the documents and trip details first

Before opening the form, prepare:

- passport data page image;
- passport-style portrait photo;
- passport number, issue date, and expiry date;
- nationality and date of birth exactly as shown in the passport;
- email address that can be accessed during the process;
- intended entry date and intended exit date;
- intended entry gate and exit gate;
- Vietnam address, such as first hotel or apartment address;
- payment card or other accepted online payment method.

Officially, the e-visa system requires a passport data-page image and a portrait photo. The portrait should show the applicant looking forward and not wearing glasses.

Practically, do not rely on old file-size advice copied from forums or old visa pages. Upload rules can appear differently across old and new systems. Use a clear image and follow the file type and size limits shown on the current official upload screen.

## Step 2: enter passport and personal information exactly

The application should match the passport and travel plan. Pay close attention to:

- surname and given name order;
- middle names;
- passport number;
- nationality;
- date of birth;
- passport issue date;
- passport expiry date;
- gender;
- email address.

The official layer is strict: missing, incorrect, or unidentified information can stop the application from being accepted. The practical layer is that many avoidable problems come from small mismatches: one wrong passport digit, a missing middle name, a date entered in the wrong order, or a photo uploaded in the wrong field.

Before payment, compare the form against the passport line by line. Do not rely on memory.

## Step 3: choose single-entry or multiple-entry

Vietnam's current e-visa framework allows e-visas for up to 90 days, with single-entry or multiple-entry options depending on the application and approval.

Choose single-entry if the trip is one continuous stay in Vietnam.

Choose multiple-entry if the plan includes leaving Vietnam and re-entering during the same visa validity period. This can matter for trips that combine Vietnam with Thailand, Cambodia, Laos, Singapore, or another regional stop.

Do not buy multiple-entry only because it feels safer. It costs more, and it still does not fix wrong dates, wrong passport details, or a route that uses the wrong checkpoint.

## Step 4: choose validity dates carefully

The e-visa has valid-from and valid-to dates. These are not decorative fields.

The official layer is that the visa result shows the dates when the holder is allowed to enter Vietnam. The practical layer is that flight changes and visa-run timing can create problems if the e-visa is not valid on the day of arrival.

Use a valid-from date that covers the planned arrival date. Use a valid-to date that covers the planned departure date, with enough margin for realistic travel changes. Do not assume the stay clock starts only when the plane lands if the approved visa shows a fixed validity window.

## Step 5: choose the entry gate and exit gate

The entry gate is the airport, land border, or seaport where the traveler plans to enter Vietnam. The exit gate is where the traveler plans to leave.

This is one of the highest-risk fields in the application.

Officially, e-visa holders must enter and exit through international checkpoints that allow e-visa use. Resolution No. 389/NQ-CP on supplementing the list of international border gates allowing foreigners to enter and exit by electronic visas, dated 2 December 2025 and effective from its signing date, supplemented the e-visa checkpoint list, so old port lists can be incomplete.

Practically, select the checkpoint that matches the actual route. For example, arriving at Noi Bai International Airport in Hanoi is different from arriving at Tan Son Nhat International Airport in Ho Chi Minh City. A land-border visa run through Moc Bai is different from flying into Da Nang.

Do not assume that a route change is harmless. If the flight, bus, border, or cruise route changes after submission, recheck the current official rules and the approved e-visa before travel.

## Step 6: pay the government fee

Vietnam e-visa fees are official government fees, not agency service fees.

Under Circular No. 28/2026/TT-BTC on fee amounts, collection and payment of fees and charges in the field of exit, entry, transit, and residence in Vietnam, effective from 1 April 2026, the relevant visa fee levels are:

| E-visa / visa type | Government fee |
|---|---:|
| Single-entry visa | US$25 |
| Multiple-entry visa with validity not exceeding 90 days | US$50 |

The fee can be non-refundable if the application is refused or the applicant is not eligible for the result. That is why the safest time to catch mistakes is before payment.

Practical payment risk: online payment can fail, remain unpaid, or create confusion if the applicant refreshes, changes browser, or tries again. If payment status is unclear, use the official status/check function or official support path. Do not submit duplicate applications casually just because a payment screen looks stuck.

## Step 7: save the registration code

After submission, save:

- registration code;
- email address used;
- date of birth used;
- payment confirmation if available;
- screenshot or PDF of the submitted information if the system provides it.

The registration code is not a minor detail. It is needed to check the result. If the code is lost, checking and correcting the application can become harder.

## Step 8: check status manually

Do not wait passively for an email.

The official status-check flow uses the registration code, email, and date of birth. The result page may show application status, denial reason, visa number, allowed entry dates, and a download/print option.

Practical rule: check the official status page manually until the result is granted and the e-visa can be downloaded. Pending, processing, unpaid, or action-needed status is not approval.

## Step 9: download and inspect the approved e-visa

Once the e-visa is granted, download it and check every field:

- full name;
- passport number;
- nationality;
- date of birth;
- visa number;
- valid-from date;
- valid-to date;
- single-entry or multiple-entry;
- entry gate;
- exit gate;
- any remarks or conditions.

If a detail is wrong, do not assume airline staff or immigration will ignore it. Check the official correction or support route before travel.

Print a paper copy and also save an offline PDF. The official e-visa instruction says approved applicants should print the e-visa to enter and exit Vietnam, and phone-only access is risky if the device battery dies, the file cannot be found, or airline or border staff ask for a paper copy.

## How long Vietnam e-visa processing takes

Officially, the e-visa instruction states that processing takes 3 working days.

Practically, that should be treated as a minimum official processing statement, not a safe travel-planning promise. For planning, treat 3-5 working days or longer around weekends, Vietnamese public holidays, correction requests, payment uncertainty, incomplete details, or high-volume periods as a safer range, and verify current portal guidance before travel.

For ordinary trips, apply before the schedule becomes tight. For visa runs, do not leave Vietnam or book a return leg around a pending e-visa unless the plan can absorb delay. A pending application is not permission to enter.

## Common rejection, delay, and amendment reasons

The official anchor is broad: missing, incorrect, or unidentified information can stop the application from being accepted.

Common practical mistake patterns include:

- passport number typo;
- name order mismatch;
- missing middle name;
- wrong nationality;
- wrong date of birth;
- wrong passport expiry date;
- blurry passport image;
- cropped or unclear portrait photo;
- portrait with glasses;
- uploaded portrait and passport image swapped;
- invalid or unrealistic Vietnam address;
- wrong intended entry date;
- wrong entry gate or exit gate;
- payment not completed;
- application left pending without manual status check.

These are practical risk patterns, not a guaranteed official rejection-code list. The safe approach is to prevent them before payment.

## When to use a paid visa agent

A paid visa agent is not required for the ordinary official self-application route.

Some travelers still use agents because they are nervous about the form, have a complex travel schedule, or need help communicating with the system. That is a commercial choice, not a government requirement.

Do not treat agency "urgent" or "emergency" claims as official processing rules. If using an agent, separate the government fee from the service fee, and remember that the final visa details still need to match the passport and route.

## Final pre-travel checklist

Before going to the airport, land border, or seaport, confirm:

- the e-visa is granted, not pending;
- the PDF is downloaded and readable;
- the name matches the passport;
- the passport number matches exactly;
- the passport used for the application is the passport being carried;
- the visa is valid on the arrival date;
- the visa is valid through the planned departure date;
- the entry gate matches the actual route;
- the exit gate is reasonable for the travel plan;
- the e-visa is single-entry or multiple-entry as needed;
- passport validity and blank pages are enough for airline and border checks;
- a printed copy is available.

Passport validity and blank-page expectations can be checked by airlines and foreign-government travel-advice systems, even when the e-visa form itself focuses on passport details. As a planning buffer, avoid traveling with a passport close to expiry or with no usable blank pages.

## FAQ

### Can an e-visa be used for a Vietnam visa run?

It can be part of a visa-run plan, but the timing risk is higher. Do not plan a same-week exit and re-entry around an application that is still pending. For route planning, use the visa-run guide: [/vietnam/visa/visa-run-guide-2026](/vietnam/visa/visa-run-guide-2026).

### Does the e-visa start when the traveler enters Vietnam?

Treat the approved valid-from and valid-to dates as the controlling window. Do not assume the validity starts only on the actual arrival date.

### Can the entry airport be changed after approval?

Do not assume that it can be changed freely. E-visa holders must use approved checkpoints, and the selected route should match the approved visa and current rules. If the route changes, recheck before travel.

### Is the 3-working-day processing time guaranteed?

No. It is the official processing statement, not a travel guarantee. Apply with extra buffer, especially around weekends, holidays, payment issues, or any correction request.

### Is a Vietnam e-visa enough to work in Vietnam?

No. An e-visa is an entry document. It is not a work permit, employer sponsorship, TRC, or long-term residence route.

### Should the e-visa be printed?

Yes. Print a paper copy and keep an offline PDF as backup. The official e-visa instruction says approved applicants should print the e-visa to enter and exit Vietnam, so the safer 2026 advice is not to rely on a phone-only copy.
