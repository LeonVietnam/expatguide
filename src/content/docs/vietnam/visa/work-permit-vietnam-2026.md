## Quick answer

A Vietnam work permit is normally the employer-sponsored document that lets a foreigner work legally for a Vietnam-based employer, unless the foreigner fits a defined exemption route.

In 2026, the current work-permit layer is built around the Labor Code No. 45/2019/QH14, effective from 1 January 2021; Decree No. 219/2025/ND-CP providing for foreign workers working in Vietnam, dated 7 August 2025 and effective from 7 August 2025; and Decision No. 346/QD-BNV on amended and supplemented administrative procedures related to foreign workers working in Vietnam in the employment sector under the state management functions of the Ministry of Home Affairs, dated 24 March 2026 and effective from its signing date.

The short version is:

| Question | Safer 2026 answer |
|---|---|
| Can a foreigner apply for a Vietnam work permit alone? | Usually no. The employer is the responsible actor, even if an agency helps prepare the file. |
| Does a work permit replace a visa? | No. A work permit is work authorization. LD visa and TRC steps are separate immigration steps. |
| Is the official work-permit timing 10 working days? | Yes, but only from a complete valid dossier. Document preparation can take longer. |
| Can a worker start while the permit is pending? | The safer planning assumption is no, unless a valid exemption basis is already in place. |
| Does exemption mean no paperwork? | Not automatically. Exemption from a work permit may still involve confirmation, employer documentation, reporting, or immigration steps. |
| Does a work permit automatically lead to a TRC? | No. It can support the LD/TRC path, but the immigration application is separate. |

This guide covers employer-sponsored Vietnam work-permit planning. It does not decide whether a remote worker serving only overseas clients is legal, illegal, exempt, or work-permit-free.

## Who this guide is for

This guide is for foreigners who have, or expect to have, a Vietnam-based employer or sponsoring entity.

It is most relevant if the reader is:

- being hired by a Vietnam company;
- transferring into a Vietnam entity;
- working for a school, language center, company, representative office, NGO, or international organization in Vietnam;
- changing employer, role, or work location inside Vietnam;
- planning the LD visa or TRC path after a work permit or exemption basis is in place.

It is less useful for:

- tourists doing no Vietnam-based work;
- remote workers with no Vietnam employer;
- investors trying to use a DT investor route instead of an employee route;
- family-based residence cases where the main status question is TT or another family route.

For general visa choices before employment sponsorship is ready, see our [Vietnam visa guide](/vietnam/visa/visa-guide-2026). For TRC planning after the employment basis is ready, see our [Vietnam TRC application guide](/vietnam/visa/trc-application-guide-2026).

## Work permit, LD visa, and TRC are three different things

A work permit answers the employment question: can this foreigner work for this Vietnam employer in this role?

An LD visa answers an immigration-entry or stay question: does the foreigner have an employment-linked visa category?

A TRC answers a longer residence question: can the foreigner receive a temporary residence card instead of relying on shorter visa stays?

These are connected, but they are not the same.

Officially, the current immigration cross-reference used for this guide is consolidated immigration-law text No. 62/VBHN-VPQH, issued on 23 March 2026. That framework distinguishes:

- LD1: foreigners working in Vietnam with confirmation that they are not subject to work-permit issuance;
- LD2: foreigners working in Vietnam who are required to have a work permit.

It also keeps LD1 / LD2 visa and TRC planning inside a time-limited immigration framework: LD1 / LD2 visas and LD1 / LD2 TRCs are capped at 2 years, and a TRC must also fit passport-validity rules.

In practice, a work permit or exemption basis can support an LD visa or TRC application, but it does not automatically issue either one. The employer or sponsor still has to handle the immigration-side application, and the passport, visa status, sponsor documents, and timing still matter.

That is why the work-permit process should be planned together with the LD/TRC path, not treated as a separate document collected at the end.

## The current 2026 legal layer

The work-permit layer changed recently enough that stale guidance is a real risk.

The official layer now starts with:

- Labor Code No. 45/2019/QH14, effective from 1 January 2021;
- Decree No. 219/2025/ND-CP providing for foreign workers working in Vietnam, dated 7 August 2025 and effective from 7 August 2025;
- Decision No. 346/QD-BNV on amended and supplemented administrative procedures related to foreign workers working in Vietnam in the employment sector under the state management functions of the Ministry of Home Affairs, dated 24 March 2026 and effective from its signing date.

Decree No. 219/2025/ND-CP replaced the foreign-worker procedure layer under older Decree No. 152/2020/ND-CP on foreign workers working in Vietnam and recruitment and management of Vietnamese workers working for foreign organizations and individuals in Vietnam, effective from 15 February 2021, as later amended by Decree No. 70/2023/ND-CP on amendments to the Government’s Decree No. 152/2020/ND-CP dated 30 December 2020 on foreign workers working in Vietnam and recruitment and management of Vietnamese workers working for foreign organizations and individuals in Vietnam, effective from 18 September 2023, from Decree No. 219/2025/ND-CP’s effective date, with transition rules for already-issued or already-filed cases.

The practical layer is messier. Older official-style pages, employer templates, and service-provider articles may still mention old Decree 152 forms, old authority names, or old processing-time language. That does not mean the old framework is safe for a new 2026 application.

For a new case, the employer should check the current Decree 219 / Decision 346 procedure and the current local procedure page before filing.

## The basic work-permit path

A normal employer-sponsored case usually moves through this sequence:

1. The employer confirms the job, work form, work location, and intended start date.
2. The employer checks whether the role fits a work-permit category or an exemption route.
3. The worker gathers documents such as passport, health certificate, criminal-record or non-prosecution proof where required, education proof, experience proof, and photos.
4. Foreign-issued documents are legalized, translated into Vietnamese, and certified unless an exemption applies.
5. The employer prepares the official dossier and submits through the relevant procedure route.
6. The authority issues the work permit, or gives a written refusal.
7. The employer and worker use the work permit or exemption basis for LD visa / TRC planning if the stay will continue.

The official work-permit issuance clock is not the same as the total preparation timeline. The 10-working-day rule applies from a complete valid dossier. The slower part may be getting the dossier complete.

## Who qualifies as manager, executive, expert, or technical worker?

A Vietnam work-permit case is not approved just because the employer uses a senior title. The job category and the evidence must line up.

### Manager

A manager route usually depends on the person holding a management position that fits the legal definition and the employer’s documents.

The practical risk is title inflation. A business card or offer letter saying “manager” may not be enough if the company documents, appointment, role description, and actual work do not support the category.

### Executive

An executive route is also narrower than ordinary business language. It can involve a branch head, representative office head, business-location head, or a person directly operating a field of an agency, organization, or enterprise.

The practical risk is evidence. The dossier should show why the person is an executive under the work-permit rules, not just a senior employee.

### Expert

The expert route is often misunderstood.

The safer reading is that the worker needs education and experience evidence that matches the job. A degree in an unrelated field, a vague experience letter, or a job title that does not match the supporting documents can create risk.

There is a narrower one-year experience route for certain priority fields or cooperation contexts. It should not be treated as a general rule that any foreigner with a degree and one year of experience qualifies as an expert.

### Technical worker

A technical-worker route depends on training and experience, or relevant experience by itself, depending on the case.

The practical issue is not only the number of years. The employer should be able to show that the experience is relevant to the intended Vietnam job.

## Required documents

The exact dossier depends on the work form, position, exemption status, and local procedure, but a work-permit case commonly involves:

- employer request / explanation form;
- valid passport or passport copy;
- health certificate or health report;
- criminal-record or non-prosecution proof where required;
- passport-style photos;
- proof of work form;
- proof of position as manager, executive, expert, or technical worker;
- employment, assignment, transfer, or appointment documents;
- business registration or other sponsor documents where required.

This list should not be read as a do-it-yourself checklist for every case. The employer has to match the documents to the exact work form and procedure.

## Education and experience proof

Education and experience proof is one of the highest-risk parts of a work-permit file.

For many expert cases, the authority will care about whether the degree, certificate, or graduation paper connects to the job. For technical-worker cases, the authority will care about whether training and experience fit the role. For manager and executive cases, company structure and appointment evidence can matter more than a degree.

A practical problem is that foreign experience letters are often written for HR purposes, not for a Vietnamese work-permit file. A vague letter that only confirms employment dates may be weaker than a letter that clearly states job title, duties, field, and duration.

If the worker previously held a Vietnam work permit or work-permit-exemption confirmation, that document may help replace experience proof in some already-worked-in-Vietnam cases. It should not be treated as a full replacement for the whole dossier.

## Legalization, translation, and the 2026 Apostille transition

Foreign-issued documents usually need more than a scan.

The official layer is that foreign documents in the dossier generally need consular legalization unless an exemption applies, and then Vietnamese translation and certification.

The practical layer is that this can become the slowest part of the case. Degrees, police checks, experience letters, appointment letters, and corporate documents may come from different countries, and each document may have its own legalization path.

There is also a 2026 date issue. Vietnam’s Apostille Convention entry into force is scheduled for 11 September 2026. Before that date, the safer assumption is that current legalization and Vietnamese translation rules still matter unless the relevant authority confirms otherwise. After that date, apostille handling should still be checked by document country, document type, and the Vietnamese authority receiving the file.

Do not assume that “apostille” automatically fixes every work-permit document in every province.

## Health certificate

The health certificate is another place where official rules and practical handling can diverge.

Officially, a health certificate or health report is part of the dossier, and foreign health certificates may be possible where recognition conditions are met.

Practically, many employers prefer a Vietnam-issued health check from an accepted hospital or clinic because it is easier to control timing, format, and language. That does not mean a foreign health certificate is never accepted, but it does mean the employer should confirm the expected format before the worker spends time and money abroad.

## Criminal-record or non-prosecution proof

A criminal-record or non-prosecution document may be required depending on the case and the procedure used.

The practical risk is timing and country history. If the worker has lived in more than one country, or needs a document from abroad, the criminal-record step can take longer than the official work-permit issuance clock.

If criminal-record processing is integrated with the work-permit procedure, the total handling time can include the criminal-record processing time plus the work-permit processing time. That is another reason not to treat “10 working days” as the full planning timeline.

## How to apply: employer, portal, and local authority

The employer is the responsible actor in a normal work-permit case.

The official layer includes online submission through the National Public Service Portal and handling by the competent authority. Decree 219 also recognizes routes such as direct submission, postal public service, hired service, or authorized submission through a local public-administration center.

The practical layer is that an agency may prepare the file, HR may coordinate the paperwork, and the local authority may still matter. This does not turn the process into a self-service foreigner application.

A safer way to think about it:

| Actor | Role |
|---|---|
| Employer / sponsor | Owns the application and confirms the job, work form, and legal basis |
| Worker | Provides personal, education, experience, health, passport, and criminal-record documents |
| Agency / service provider | May help prepare or submit documents, but does not replace employer responsibility |
| Competent authority | Reviews the dossier and issues or refuses the permit |
| Immigration authority | Handles LD visa / TRC steps separately after the work basis exists |

The current authority should not be written as one universal office name for every case. The safer wording is provincial People’s Committee or delegated competent authority, checked through the relevant local procedure.

## Processing time and planning buffer

Officially, the employer files the work-permit dossier within 60 days and not less than 10 days before the expected work start date.

Officially, the issuance stage is 10 working days from receipt of a complete valid dossier. If the application is refused, the authority must respond in writing within the official refusal period.

Practically, the total timeline can be longer because the official clock starts only after the dossier is complete enough to be accepted. Before that point, the worker and employer may still be handling:

- foreign document legalization;
- Vietnamese translation and certification;
- health certificate;
- criminal-record or non-prosecution proof;
- experience letters;
- degree or training proof;
- employer forms and business documents;
- corrections requested by the reviewing authority.

For planning, treat the official 10-working-day clock as only one stage. A simple case with documents already in the right format may still need about 3–4 weeks end to end, while a case that needs overseas police checks, foreign-document legalization, translation, or employer corrections may need 4–8 weeks or more before the worker has a usable permit basis. This is a planning band, not a guaranteed official processing time.

A practical planning rule is to avoid making the work start date depend on the official 10-working-day clock alone. Build time for document preparation before the formal filing window.

## Fees

Do not assume one national work-permit fee applies everywhere.

The procedure layer points to province-level fee authority, so the employer should check the current provincial procedure page or local competent authority. Some older pages or service-provider articles may show fixed examples, but those should not be treated as a universal 2026 national fee.

For planning, treat the government work-permit issuance fee as a local administrative fee, usually in the hundreds of thousands of dong rather than a multi-million-dong charge. A cautious planning band for the government issuance fee is about VND 400,000–1,000,000 for a new permit, with current provincial examples commonly around VND 600,000. Online submission discounts or local waiver policies can make the payable amount lower in some provinces.

This government fee is separate from practical preparation costs such as health checks, criminal-record documents, translation, notarization, legalization, courier costs, or paid service-provider support.

For the reader, the safer question to ask the employer is:

“Which province is filing the work permit, what is the current local government fee, and which preparation costs are paid by the employer?”

## Work-permit exemption

Some foreigners are exempt from work-permit issuance. Examples can include certain capital contributors, short-term cases, intra-company transferees under defined conditions, foreign lawyers with the relevant Vietnamese practicing certificate, and other categories listed by law.

The important distinction is this:

Exempt from a work permit does not always mean exempt from paperwork.

A worker may still need confirmation, proof, employer reporting, or an immigration step. The employer should confirm whether the case needs a work-permit-exemption confirmation or falls into a narrower category where confirmation procedure is not required.

This matters for LD status too. LD1 is linked to foreigners working in Vietnam with confirmation that they are not subject to work-permit issuance. LD2 is linked to foreigners who are required to have work permits.

## Can you work while the work permit is pending?

The safer planning assumption is no.

Officially, a foreigner who is required to have a work permit should not treat a pending application as work authorization. The Labor Code also creates serious consequences for working without the required permit.

Practically, some employers or recruiters may say the worker can begin because “the file is in process,” “the DN visa is enough,” or “the permit is coming soon.” Those assurances should not be treated as legal authorization.

A safer question to ask before starting work is:

“Has the employer received the work permit or confirmed exemption basis for this exact worker, role, employer, and work location?”

## How the work permit connects to LD visa

The work permit does not replace the visa.

If the worker needs an employment-linked immigration category, the work-permit or exemption basis may support the LD route:

- LD1 is for foreigners working in Vietnam with confirmation that they are not subject to work-permit issuance.
- LD2 is for foreigners working in Vietnam who are required to have a work permit.

The visa step still depends on the sponsor, passport, current status, and immigration application. A worker entering on an e-visa or visa exemption should not assume that entry status allows work.

## How the work permit connects to TRC

A work permit or exemption basis can support a TRC path, but it does not automatically create a TRC.

For an employment-linked TRC, the sponsor still needs to handle the immigration application. The passport validity, LD category, sponsor documents, current status, and timing all matter.

Under consolidated immigration-law text No. 62/VBHN-VPQH, issued on 23 March 2026, LD1 / LD2 TRCs are capped at 2 years, and the card also has to fit passport-validity rules. In practice, the TRC often ends earlier than the maximum if the passport, work permit, labor contract, or sponsor basis expires earlier.

For the full residence-card process, see our [Vietnam TRC application guide](/vietnam/visa/trc-application-guide-2026). If the reader already has a card and is planning the next cycle, see our [Vietnam TRC renewal guide](/vietnam/visa/trc-renewal-vietnam-2026).

## Changing employer, role, or province

A Vietnam work permit should be treated as tied to the employer, role, work form, and supporting documents.

If the worker changes employer, the new employer should not assume the old permit can simply be reused.

If the worker keeps the same employer but changes job position or work form, a new or updated procedure may be needed.

If the worker works in multiple provinces for the same employer, the employer may need to handle notification or local requirements before the worker starts in another province.

The practical rule is simple: if the real work no longer matches the permit basis, ask before assuming the permit still protects the worker.

## Common traps

### Trap 1: “The company says the business visa is enough”

A business visa or DN-style entry is not the same as a work permit. It may support business activities in some contexts, but it should not be treated as permission to work as an employee if a work permit is required.

### Trap 2: “The work permit is approved, so the TRC is automatic”

The work permit may support the TRC path. It does not issue the TRC by itself.

### Trap 3: “The title says manager, so the manager category is fine”

The authority looks at documents, not only a job title. Company structure, appointment papers, duties, and experience may matter.

### Trap 4: “Exempt means no process”

Exemption can reduce the work-permit requirement, but it may still involve confirmation, reporting, sponsor documents, or immigration steps.

### Trap 5: “The 10-day timeline means the whole case takes 10 days”

The 10-working-day clock is an official decision stage from a complete valid dossier. It does not include every document-preparation step.

### Trap 6: “Old Decree 152 guidance is still good enough”

Older pages may remain online under Decree No. 152/2020/ND-CP on foreign workers working in Vietnam and recruitment and management of Vietnamese workers working for foreign organizations and individuals in Vietnam, effective from 15 February 2021. New 2026 cases should be checked against Decree No. 219/2025/ND-CP and current procedure pages.

## Questions to ask the employer before accepting the job

Before relying on a Vietnam job offer, ask:

1. Which Vietnam legal entity will sponsor the work permit?
2. Which province will file the application?
3. Which category will the employer use: manager, executive, expert, technical worker, or exemption?
4. Which documents does the employer need from the worker?
5. Do any documents need legalization or Vietnamese translation?
6. Is a criminal-record or non-prosecution document needed?
7. Will the employer arrange the health certificate?
8. What start date is safe if the work permit is not issued yet?
9. Will the employer support LD visa and TRC steps after the work permit?
10. What happens if the permit or TRC timing slips?

If the employer cannot answer these questions, the risk is not only paperwork delay. It may mean the employer has not mapped the legal basis for the role.

## FAQ

### Can I apply for a Vietnam work permit myself?

Usually, no. A normal work-permit application is employer-sponsored. The worker provides personal and qualification documents, but the employer is the responsible actor.

### Can I work in Vietnam on an e-visa?

An e-visa is an entry document. It should not be treated as work authorization for a Vietnam employer. If the reader is being hired by a Vietnam employer, the employer should check the work-permit or exemption path.

### Can I work on a 45-day visa exemption?

A 45-day visa exemption is a short-entry basis for eligible passports. It does not by itself create work authorization. See the separate [45-day visa exemption guide](/vietnam/visa/45-day-visa-exemption-vietnam-2026) for entry planning.

### Can I start work while the permit is being processed?

The safer assumption is no, unless the employer already has a valid exemption basis for that exact case. A pending application, business visa, or employer reassurance should not be treated as permission to work.

### How long does a Vietnam work permit take?

Officially, the issuance stage is measured from a complete valid dossier. The 2026 procedure anchor uses a 10-working-day issuance period from that point. In practice, the total case can take longer because document preparation, legalization, translation, health checks, criminal-record proof, and employer corrections may happen first.

### Does a work permit give me a TRC?

No. A work permit or exemption basis can support the LD/TRC path, but the TRC application is separate.

### Can I change employers with the same work permit?

Do not assume that. A Vietnam work permit is tied to the employer and work basis. A new employer normally needs its own work-permit or exemption procedure.

### Do all foreign documents need legalization?

Foreign documents generally need legalization unless an exemption applies, followed by Vietnamese translation and certification. Because Vietnam’s Apostille Convention entry into force is scheduled for 11 September 2026, document handling in late 2026 should be checked carefully by document country and receiving authority.

### Is a foreign health certificate accepted?

It may be possible where recognition conditions are met, but many employers prefer a Vietnam-issued health check because it is easier to control format and timing. The employer should confirm before the worker relies on a foreign certificate.

### What if my employer says they have “done this many times”?

That helps only if the employer can map the current 2026 procedure, the sponsoring entity, the job category, the document list, and the LD/TRC plan. Experience with old Decree 152 paperwork is not enough by itself for a new 2026 case.
