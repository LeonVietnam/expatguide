## Short answer

Vietnam's investor visa is the **DT visa** category. It is for foreigners who can document investment in Vietnam, not for people who simply want a long stay without an investment basis.

The key point in 2026 is that **DT1, DT2, and DT3 are the investor categories that matter most for longer residence planning**. DT4 exists, but it is the low-tier investor category for investment below **VND 3 billion**, and it should not be treated as a strong residence route.

In practical terms:

- **DT1** is for documented investment from **VND 100 billion** or certain qualifying investment-incentive cases.
- **DT2** is for documented investment from **VND 50 billion to under VND 100 billion**, or certain encouraged-sector cases.
- **DT3** is for documented investment from **VND 3 billion to under VND 50 billion**.
- **DT4** is for documented investment below **VND 3 billion**.
- **DT4 is the trap**: it can be an investor visa category, but it is not listed in the official DT-based Temporary Residence Card categories.

If the goal is a stable long-term Vietnam residence path, DT4 is usually the wrong category to rely on without a second legal basis. Compare it against DT3, LD/work-based status, TT family status, or a simpler short-stay route first.

## What a Vietnam investor visa is

A Vietnam investor visa uses the **DT** symbol. It is issued to foreign investors in Vietnam and representatives of foreign organizations investing in Vietnam.

The DT category sits inside Vietnam's immigration law framework: **Law No. 47/2014/QH13 on Entry, Exit, Transit, and Residence of Foreigners in Vietnam, effective from 1 January 2015**, as amended by **Law No. 51/2019/QH14 on amendments to the Law on Entry, Exit, Transit, and Residence of Foreigners in Vietnam, effective from 1 July 2020**, and **Law No. 23/2023/QH15 on amendments to the Law on Exit and Entry of Vietnamese Citizens and the Law on Entry, Exit, Transit, and Residence of Foreigners in Vietnam, effective from 15 August 2023**.

For readers, the legal citation matters because many online pages compress DT into one generic "investor visa." That is not safe enough. The category is split into DT1, DT2, DT3, and DT4, and the split affects validity, residence-card planning, family planning, and whether the category is worth pursuing.

A DT visa is also not the same thing as a Temporary Residence Card, usually shortened to TRC. A visa can support entry and stay. A TRC is a stronger residence document. DT1, DT2, and DT3 can support DT-based TRC planning if the documents fit. DT4 should be treated separately.

## DT1, DT2, DT3, and DT4 compared

| DT category | Investment / legal basis | Visa maximum validity | Residence planning position |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| DT1 | Foreign investor or representative of a foreign investing organization with contributed capital from **VND 100 billion**, or investment in qualifying investment-incentive sectors / areas decided by the Government | Up to **5 years** | Strongest DT-based residence category; DT1 TRC can be up to 10 years |
| DT2 | Contributed capital from **VND 50 billion to under VND 100 billion**, or investment in qualifying encouraged development sectors decided by the Government | Up to **5 years** | Strong DT-based residence category; DT2 TRC can be up to 5 years |
| DT3 | Contributed capital from **VND 3 billion to under VND 50 billion** | Up to **3 years** | Lowest DT category clearly listed for DT-based TRC planning; DT3 TRC can be up to 3 years |
| DT4 | Contributed capital below **VND 3 billion** | Up to **1 year** | Weak residence path; DT4 is not listed as a DT-based TRC category |

Use the VND thresholds as the legal anchor. USD equivalents should only be treated as rough currency conversions, not as legal thresholds.

The "up to" wording is also important. A category may have a statutory maximum, but actual issuance can still be limited by the passport, documents, approval route, and case facts.

## The DT4 low-tier trap

DT4 is the investor-visa category for documented investment below **VND 3 billion**. That makes it attractive on paper, especially for small company owners, founders, and foreigners who hear that "opening a company" can create a long-stay solution.

The official layer is narrower: DT4 exists as an investor visa category, and DT4 visa validity is capped at **up to one year**. But the official Temporary Residence Card framework lists **DT1, DT2, and DT3**, not DT4, as DT-based TRC categories.

The practical layer is where the trap appears. Some service pages blur the words "investor visa," "temporary residence," and "residence card." A reader may think any DT investor status can become a TRC. That is not the safe reading. For residence planning, DT4 is much weaker than DT3 because DT4 is not listed as a DT-based TRC category.

The safe way to think about DT4 is this:

| Reader goal | Is DT4 a good fit? | Why |
|---|---:|---|
| Short investor-linked stay | Conditional | It may fit if the investment documents are real and the visa validity is enough |
| Long-term residence stability | Usually no | DT4 is not listed as a DT-based TRC category |
| Spouse / child planning through TT | Usually no | TT family basis lists DT1, DT2, and DT3, not DT4 |
| Work authorization | Not enough information | Work-permit and work-permit-exemption rules need separate verification |
| Upgrade path to stronger residence | Conditional | The reader may need DT3+, LD, TT, or another lawful basis |

Do not pay for a DT4 "residence-card solution" unless the provider can explain the exact legal basis and category. The safer assumption is that DT4 is a visa category, not a reliable TRC path.

## DT visa vs DT-based TRC

A DT visa and a DT-based TRC solve different problems.

| Document | What it does | DT categories that matter |
|---|---|---|
| DT visa | Supports entry and stay for a documented investor purpose | DT1, DT2, DT3, DT4 |
| DT-based TRC | Supports longer residence as a temporary residence card | DT1, DT2, DT3 |
| TT family route connected to investor status | Can matter for spouse / children under 18 of certain status holders | DT1, DT2, DT3, not DT4 |

For DT-based TRC planning:

- DT1 TRC can be valid for up to **10 years**.
- DT2 TRC can be valid for up to **5 years**.
- DT3 TRC can be valid for up to **3 years**.
- DT4 is not listed in the DT-based TRC categories.

This does not mean every DT1, DT2, or DT3 holder automatically receives a TRC. The sponsor, passport validity, investment documents, residence documents, and immigration-office review still matter.

## Required documents: official basis vs practical file

The official layer is simple but not complete enough for planning. Vietnam's immigration law requires an investor-purpose applicant to have documents proving investment in Vietnam under the investment-law framework, plus the normal immigration conditions such as passport / travel document, invitation or sponsorship where required, and no entry-suspension issue.

The practical layer is more document-heavy. In a real DT file, the sponsor company or invested entity may be asked to prepare several categories of documents.

| Document category | Why it matters | How to treat it |
|---|---|---|
| Passport | Identity and validity basis | Required; longer visa or TRC validity can be limited by passport validity |
| Investment proof | Shows the reader is actually an investor | Core DT requirement |
| Company / entity documents | Shows the Vietnamese sponsor or invested entity exists and can support the file | Commonly needed; exact list varies |
| Investment registration documents, where applicable | Supports project or foreign-investor status | Depends on the investment form |
| Enterprise registration documents | Supports company ownership / sponsor identity | Commonly requested in company-based cases |
| Capital-contribution or ownership evidence | Supports DT1-DT4 threshold classification | Important; do not rely on informal claims |
| Seal / signature introduction or sponsor-side authority record | Supports sponsor submission | May be required by procedure or office practice |
| Visa / TRC application form and photo | Standard immigration file components | Use the current official form, not a stale downloaded template |
| Temporary residence / address record | May be relevant for in-country procedures | Confirm with the receiving office |
| Power of attorney / representative documents | Relevant if a lawyer, agent, or company staff handles filing | Case-specific |

This list should be treated as a planning checklist, not as a universal legal list for every office. The exact file should be checked against the current immigration procedure, the receiving office, or qualified counsel before submission.

## Where the application is handled

A DT case is not usually like an e-visa application where the foreigner simply uploads documents alone and waits.

The official layer makes the inviting or guaranteeing organization important. In many cases, the Vietnamese organization, invested company, or sponsor-side representative is the party preparing or submitting the request to immigration authorities. Electronic routes may exist for eligible organizations, but those routes depend on the organization's account and electronic-signature setup.

The practical layer is that the file may involve one or more of these landing points:

- the Vietnam Immigration Department;
- the immigration management office where the sponsor or invested entity is based;
- an official public-service portal route for eligible filings;
- a Vietnamese embassy or consulate if the visa is issued outside Vietnam;
- sponsor-side handling by company staff, lawyer, or immigration service provider.

Do not assume that every DT case can be completed by the foreign investor alone online. Also do not assume that an agent can bypass the statutory eligibility rules. Agents and lawyers can help prepare a file, but they cannot turn a non-qualifying investment into DT1, DT2, or DT3 status.

For readers who only need a short stay while documents are being prepared, the better comparison may be an e-visa or exemption route. ## Fees in 2026

The current official fee anchor is **Circular No. 28/2026/TT-BTC regulating fee and charge amounts, collection, payment, management, and use in the field of exit, entry, transit, and residence in Vietnam, effective from 1 April 2026**.

For investor-visa planning, separate three cost layers:

| Cost layer | What it includes | Planning note |
|---|---|---|
| Government visa / residence fee | Official visa or TRC fee charged by the competent authority | Use Circular No. 28/2026/TT-BTC as the 2026 source |
| Document costs | Translation, notarization, legalization, photos, company extracts, courier, copies | Varies by document and case |
| Professional / corporate setup costs | Lawyer, agent, accountant, company setup, investment registration, capital contribution support | Not covered by the government visa fee |

Government fee bands under Circular No. 28/2026/TT-BTC include:

| Official fee item | Fee |
|---|---:|
| Single-entry visa | **US$25** |
| Multiple-entry visa up to 90 days | **US$50** |
| Multiple-entry visa over 90 days to 180 days | **US$95** |
| Multiple-entry visa over 180 days to 1 year | **US$135** |
| Multiple-entry visa over 1 year to 2 years | **US$145** |
| Multiple-entry visa over 2 years to 5 years | **US$155** |
| Multiple-entry visa over 5 years to 10 years | **US$165** |
| TRC up to 2 years | **US$145** |
| TRC over 2 years to 5 years | **US$155** |
| TRC over 5 years to 10 years | **US$165** |

These are official government fee bands, not the total cost of getting investor status. A reader who still needs company setup, investment registration, capital-contribution evidence, translations, notarization, legal review, or agent help should expect additional costs.

Be careful with older articles and provider pages. Many pages still repeat older fee tables from before April 2026.

## Timeline: official processing vs realistic planning

The official processing anchor can be short after a complete file is received. Relevant immigration-law provisions include 5-working-day decision windows for certain visa and TRC procedures after the competent authority receives the required documents.

That is not the same as saying "a Vietnam investor visa takes five days."

A safer planning view is:

| Stage | Typical planning risk | Why it can take longer |
|---|---|---|
| Confirm DT category | Medium | The investment amount and legal documents must support DT1, DT2, DT3, or DT4 |
| Prepare sponsor file | Medium to high | Company documents, seal/signature records, representative authority, and investment proof may need correction |
| Submit visa request / immigration file | Medium | Route depends on sponsor, location, and whether the file is in-country or overseas |
| Official review after sufficient dossier | Lower, if the file is complete | Official anchor can be around 5 working days for relevant procedures |
| Visa issuance / stamping / result collection | Medium | Overseas mission, local office, or route-specific steps may add time |
| TRC application, if eligible | Medium to high | DT1-DT3 only; separate file and residence-card review |

For practical planning, do not schedule around the official 5-working-day anchor unless the sponsor file and investment proof are already complete. If the current visa is close to expiry, build in a buffer or compare another lawful short-stay route first. A visa run should be treated as a fallback, not as an investor-residence strategy; see our [Vietnam visa run guide](/vietnam/visa/visa-run-guide-2026).

## DT vs other Vietnam residence paths

A DT visa is not always the best Vietnam path. The better route depends on the reader's real legal basis.

| Reader goal | Route to compare | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Short stay while exploring Vietnam | 45-day exemption or e-visa | Simpler if the reader is not yet an investor |
| Market research or business meetings | E-visa or DN route, depending on documents | Do not force DT before investment documents exist |
| Long-term stay through employment | LD / work-based route | DT does not replace work-permit analysis |
| Long-term stay through family | TT route | More relevant if family basis exists |
| Long-term stay through documented investment | DT3, DT2, or DT1 | Stronger than DT4 for residence planning |
| Existing TRC holder | TRC renewal or category change | Do not switch to DT unless it improves the legal basis |
| Low-capital company owner | DT4 plus alternative plan | DT4 may be a visa category, but it is weak as a residence strategy |

For most readers, the decision is not "Can an agent get a DT visa?" The better question is: **Does the investment category support the residence outcome the reader actually needs?**

## Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: treating DT4 as a cheap TRC route

DT4 is the low-tier investor visa category. It is not listed as a DT-based TRC category. If stable residence is the goal, DT4 should trigger caution, not confidence.

### Mistake 2: confusing visa validity with TRC validity

DT visa maximums and DT-based TRC maximums are different. DT1 and DT2 visas can be valid up to 5 years, but a DT1 TRC can be up to 10 years. DT4 can have a visa validity cap of up to 1 year, but that does not make it a TRC category.

### Mistake 3: assuming company setup alone is enough

The law focuses on documented investment. A company name, informal agreement, or planned investment may not be enough. The file needs investment documents that support the category being requested.

### Mistake 4: relying on provider tables without checking the law

Service-provider pages can be useful for practical preparation, but they are not the authority for DT thresholds, TRC eligibility, or official government fees. If a provider page says something different from the statutory DT table or TRC category list, treat it as a warning sign.

### Mistake 5: treating five working days as the whole timeline

Five working days may be an official processing anchor after sufficient documents are received. The real delay is often before that: sponsor file, investment evidence, form correction, and office-specific instructions.

### Mistake 6: assuming DT answers work authorization

DT status is an immigration category. It does not automatically answer every work-permit or work-permit-exemption question. If the reader will actively work, manage, or be employed in Vietnam, that question needs separate legal verification.

## Application checklist

Before pursuing a Vietnam investor visa, confirm these points:

- The investment amount clearly fits DT1, DT2, DT3, or DT4.
- The investment is documented under the current Vietnam investment-law framework.
- The Vietnamese sponsor or invested entity can support the immigration file.
- The passport has enough remaining validity for the requested visa or TRC period.
- The reader understands the difference between DT visa validity and DT-based TRC validity.
- DT4 is not being treated as a TRC route.
- The official government fee source is current for 2026.
- Any service provider can explain the exact legal category, not just sell an "investor visa package."
- The current visa or stay period has enough buffer for document preparation.
- There is a fallback route if the DT file cannot be completed in time.

## Bottom line

A Vietnam investor visa can be useful when the investment is real, documented, and matched to the correct DT category.

For long-term residence planning, the practical cutoff is not just "Do you have a company?" It is whether the documents support **DT1, DT2, or DT3**. DT4 may exist as the low-tier investor visa, but it is the category most likely to disappoint readers who are actually trying to build a stable residence plan.

The safest approach is to decide in this order:

1. Confirm the documented investment amount.
2. Match it to DT1, DT2, DT3, or DT4.
3. Separate visa planning from TRC planning.
4. Treat DT4 as weak for residence.
5. Compare other lawful routes before spending money on a package that does not solve the real problem.
