## The short answer

If the apartment already has a washer or washer-dryer, use that first. It is usually the cheapest option and gives the most control over heat, detergent, and load separation.

For routine clothes and bedding, a neighborhood giặt ủi shop is usually the best value. For time-saving in bigger cities, pickup-delivery can make sense, but it costs more and the exact turnaround depends on the operator and cutoff time. For suits, silk, wool, embellished dresses, and other structured or delicate items, use dry cleaning rather than standard wash-and-fold.

Self-service laundromats exist in some cities, but they are still a niche option rather than the default answer nationwide.

*Prices and turnaround times below are approximate published examples from named operators as of April 2026. Real prices vary by city, load size, item type, delivery distance, holiday periods, and whether a service is standard, urgent, or premium.*

## Start with the apartment or building

Before searching for an outside laundry service, check the unit, building, and reception desk.

If the apartment has its own washer or washer-dryer, there is usually no separate laundry fee. The real cost is detergent plus whatever the rental setup does with electricity and water. That is usually the best answer for small weekly loads, gym clothes, and anything that should not be mixed with other items.

If the building includes shared machine access or laundry as part of the rent, the point-of-use cost can be effectively zero. If reception sends laundry out instead, treat it like an outside service and ask for the current price and return time before using it.

If the building has shared machines, use those before paying a pickup service. If the building is a serviced apartment, reception or housekeeping may either:
- point residents to shared machines
- arrange external laundry on request
- include some laundry support in the rent
- offer washer-dryer access inside the unit

Do not assume the listing description tells the whole story. Ask:
- Is there a washer in the unit or only in the building?
- Is dryer access included?
- Is laundry bundled into rent or charged separately?
- If reception sends laundry out, what is the current price and return time?

This matters for move-in checks too. If a rental ad promised amenities that turn out to be different on arrival, the same verification habit used in [Avoid Rental Scams in Vietnam](/vietnam/housing/avoid-rental-scams-vietnam-2026) helps here as well.

## Neighborhood giặt ủi shops: usually the best value for normal loads

For most expats, this is the default paid option for daily clothes, towels, and bedding.

The main reason is price. Public examples show standard clothes in the low tens of thousands of VND, but the model varies by shop:
- 30 Laundry in Hue shows 45,000 VND for under 3kg and 15,000 VND/kg above 3kg
- the same operator shows bedding and towels around 20,000-25,000 VND/kg depending on thickness
- urgent tourist-area service in Da Nang can be higher, with one operator advertising 30,000 VND/kg for 3-hour service

That does not mean 15,000 VND/kg is the Vietnam-wide price. It means local giặt ủi is usually cheap, but the final bill depends on the city, the minimum-load rule, and whether the shop is selling speed, delivery, or English-friendly convenience.

What this option is best for:
- T-shirts, shorts, jeans, socks, underwear, towels
- larger loads that are annoying to do by hand
- bedding and towels when the shop has industrial machines

What to watch:
- ask whether whites and darks are washed together
- ask whether there is a separate-color fee
- ask whether the quoted price is per kg, under-minimum flat fee, or by load
- ask what time pickup is possible, not just when the shop closes

A local shop is often the best answer when the goal is cheap and good enough, not premium garment care.

## Pickup-delivery laundry: pay more to save time

This option makes more sense in HCMC, Hanoi, Da Nang, and a few other larger markets than in smaller cities.

There are two common versions:
- app-based or home-service platforms
- premium laundry operators with their own pickup network

Public examples show why this tier costs more than a neighborhood drop-off:
- bTaskee publishes a 40,000 VND pickup-and-delivery fee per order and a 50% urgent surcharge
- Heramo in HCMC markets a 150,000 VND wash-dry package up to 6kg, with 24-hour completion for that service
- 30 Laundry in Hue also offers pickup-delivery, which shows that this model is not limited to the two largest cities, but service depth is still operator- and city-specific

This option is best for:
- busy workweeks
- families who want one scheduled collection instead of multiple shop runs
- short stays in bigger cities where time matters more than squeezing the last bit of value out of each kg

This option is a weaker fit when:
- the apartment already has a good washer
- there is a cheap giặt ủi shop on the same street
- the load is small enough that the delivery fee changes the math

The practical distinction here is not official rules. It is advertised service versus real execution. A provider may publish 24-hour service, but cutoff time, district, weather, and order volume still matter. Confirm the actual return window before handing over office wear or travel-day clothes.

For booking, larger operators may use their app, website, hotline, or WhatsApp. That is one reason it helps to have [Vietnam Essential Apps](/vietnam/daily-life/vietnam-essential-apps-2026) and local messaging set up early.

## Dry cleaning: use it for the clothes that actually need it

Dry cleaning should not be the default answer for weekly laundry. It is the better choice for items that can lose shape, texture, or finish in a standard wash-dry cycle.

Public item-by-item menus show the pricing logic clearly:
- shirts around 30,000 VND
- dress pants around 35,000 VND
- vest around 80,000 VND
- suit sets around 100,000-110,000 VND
- coats around 160,000-200,000 VND
- dresses can run higher depending on construction and fabric

This option is the better fit for:
- suits, blazers, coats
- silk, wool, lace, embellished dresses
- formalwear and officewear that needs shape retention
- items with lining, structure, or trim that should not go into a routine mixed load

Premium operators also tend to separate dry cleaning from normal laundry in their turnaround. Heramo, for example, markets about 72 hours for dry cleaning rather than its faster 24-hour wash-dry service.

The mistake to avoid is paying dry-cleaning prices for everyday gym clothes, cotton sleepwear, or basic T-shirts that a normal wash would handle fine.

## Self-service laundromats: useful, but not the default nationwide answer

Self-service laundromats exist in at least some urban areas, and they are worth knowing about if the apartment has no washer and local shop quality is poor.

Koi Wash in Hanoi shows the rough model:
- about 60,000 VND for a 10.5kg wash
- about 50,000 VND for a 15kg dry
- about 30 minutes per wash or dry cycle
- detergent and softener handled automatically
- payment by cash token, QR, or card depending on the setup

This option is most useful for:
- bulky items
- short stays with no washer in the room
- people who want full control but do not have their own machine
- loads that need drying during rainy periods

The limitation is availability. Self-service laundry is still a city-specific option, not something to assume exists in every neighborhood.

## Where to look first in bigger cities and smaller cities

In HCMC, the public source base is strongest for premium pickup-delivery and operator-run laundry. In Hanoi, the visible public options include serviced apartments with washer-dryers and at least some self-service laundromat coverage. In Da Nang, fast tourist-area laundry and WhatsApp-friendly shops are easier to find than in many smaller cities.

In smaller cities and quieter neighborhoods, the usual search order is simpler:
1. check the apartment or reception
2. search nearby for giặt ủi
3. ask the landlord, hotel desk, or guesthouse staff
4. only assume pickup-delivery exists after confirming a local operator

## Which option makes sense for each use case

### Daily clothes
Use the in-unit washer first. If there is no machine, use a nearby giặt ủi shop.

### Bedding and towels
A local giặt ủi shop or self-service industrial machine usually makes more sense than a small home washer.

### Office wear
Use a good local laundry for standard shirts and simple workwear. Move up to dry cleaning for jackets, suit pants, structured dresses, or anything with delicate fabric.

### Formalwear and delicate fabrics
Use dry cleaning.

### Busy weeks in major cities
Use pickup-delivery if the time saved matters more than the higher bill.

### Short stays or serviced apartments
Check the building first. A shared washer or reception-arranged service can make outside searching unnecessary.

## What to ask before handing over clothes

A two-minute check avoids most problems:
- Is the price per kg, per load, or per item?
- Is there a minimum-load fee?
- Are whites and darks washed separately?
- Is there an extra charge for separate-color washing?
- What is the return time for standard service?
- What is the express fee?
- Is pickup-delivery included, charged separately, or free above a minimum order?
- Do they return clothes folded, hung, or both?
- How do they handle stains, delicate fabrics, or missing buttons?

For routine budgeting, laundry is one of those small recurring costs that is easy to ignore until it adds up. [Cost of Living in Vietnam](/vietnam/daily-life/cost-of-living-vietnam-2026) is a useful place to benchmark how often paying for convenience still fits the monthly budget.

## A simple decision rule

Use this order:
1. in-unit washer or shared building machine if the setup is good
2. neighborhood giặt ủi for cheap routine loads
3. pickup-delivery when time matters more than price
4. dry cleaning for garments that actually need special care
5. self-service laundromat when a city has one and full control matters

If a service has to promise very fast turnaround, English-friendly communication, hotel pickup, and separate handling, the price usually moves up. If the goal is simply clean everyday clothes, the local giặt ủi shop often remains the best value.
