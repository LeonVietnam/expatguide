## The short answer

If the job is normal apartment upkeep, start with an app-based hourly cleaner. If the same home needs cleaning every week or every two weeks, recurring cleaning is usually the better fit. If the home has built-up grease, dust, post-move mess, or a long-neglected kitchen or bathroom, book deep cleaning instead of hoping a normal short session will cover it.

In most Vietnam cities, the real decision is not just which brand to pick. It is which service model matches the job. As a rough 2026 public-price anchor, standard app-booked home cleaning commonly runs around **170,000-250,000 VND** for **2 hours** and around **165,000-324,000 VND** for **3 hours**, while some **4-hour** bookings can run higher depending on platform and pricing structure, and deep cleaning usually moves into team-based or app-quoted pricing and costs more.

*Public provider pricing varies by city, time slot, holiday demand, apartment size, and add-ons. Check the live quote in-app before confirming.*

## The four real options most expats will compare

### 1) App-based hourly cleaning

This is the best starting point for most expats in studios, one-bedrooms, and standard two-bedroom apartments.

A normal hourly booking is usually the right fit when the home needs:
- floors, dusting, bathroom cleaning, and a kitchen reset
- dishes, light tidying, and routine upkeep
- a one-off clean before guests, after a busy week, or before returning from travel

The strongest public consumer booking anchors in Vietnam right now are **bTaskee** and **JupViec**. Both clearly separate normal cleaning from heavier service types, and both let the customer see a quote inside the app before confirming.

### 2) Recurring cleaning

Recurring cleaning makes more sense when the same home needs regular upkeep and the goal is consistency, not just one emergency booking.

This is often the better fit for:
- busy couples
- families with children
- homes with pets
- remote workers who do not want chores stacking up every week

Public platform materials support repeat scheduling and, in some cases, preferred cleaner handling. In practice, that helps with continuity, but it should be treated as a **preference rather than a guarantee**. If having the same cleaner matters, confirm that point before assuming it.

### 3) Deep cleaning

Deep cleaning is the better booking when the home needs more than routine upkeep.

That usually means:
- move-in or move-out cleanup
- a unit that has been empty for a while
- kitchen grease buildup
- bathroom scale and heavier grime
- dust after repairs or furniture work
- a home where a normal 2-4 hour session is clearly too small for the list

Published provider structures show why this should be treated as a separate choice. Some public deep-cleaning formats currently use **at least two workers**, and some quote the job as a team-based package rather than as one cleaner doing a short shift.

### 4) An independent cleaner found through referrals

This route can work well when the cleaner already comes recommended by a landlord, building receptionist, neighbor, or another long-term tenant.

It can also be the hardest route for a new arrival to judge because:
- service scope is often agreed in chat or in person, not through a fixed booking flow
- replacement, complaint handling, and timing are less structured
- first-time trust is more dependent on the referral itself

For a first booking in Vietnam, many expats will find the app or company route easier to manage. The independent route tends to work better once there is already a trusted local contact.

## What standard hourly cleaning usually covers

For most apartments, standard cleaning is the right fit when the goal is regular upkeep, not a full reset.

A normal session will usually cover the obvious daily-life cleaning work:
- sweeping, mopping, dusting
- bathroom cleaning
- kitchen surface cleaning
- dishes and light tidying
- basic room-by-room upkeep

Do not assume that every add-on sits inside the base clean. Window exteriors, mattress or sofa shampooing, curtain washing, appliance detailing, and heavy balcony cleanup are often handled separately. Laundry is also its own decision in many homes; see [Laundry in Vietnam for Expats](/vietnam/daily-life/laundry-vietnam-expats-2026). AC servicing is separate again; see [Air Conditioning in Vietnam Apartments](/vietnam/housing/air-conditioning-vietnam-apartments-2026).

## When recurring cleaning is better than rebooking each time

Recurring cleaning is usually the better choice when the apartment gets messy in predictable cycles.

That includes homes where:
- the kitchen and bathrooms slip quickly after a few days
- pet hair or dust returns fast
- the household wants a fixed weekly rhythm
- someone works from home and does not want cleaning to become a weekend job

The practical advantage is not just price. It is routine. A repeat schedule reduces the friction of rebooking, re-explaining the apartment, and waiting until the place feels bad enough to justify another one-off visit.

Still, platform wording and real execution are not identical here. A platform may support repeat schedules, favorite cleaners, or subscription-style booking, but the same-person result still depends on availability. Treat recurring service as a better path to consistency, not as an automatic same-cleaner promise.

## When to book deep cleaning instead of normal cleaning

Book deep cleaning first when the home needs a reset, not maintenance.

That is usually the right call when:
- the unit is just being handed over
- the last tenant left behind visible buildup
- the kitchen and bathrooms need detail work in corners, vents, cabinets, or glass
- the job would obviously run beyond a normal short session

If the list already sounds like "everything in the home needs attention," a normal clean is often the wrong booking. It is better to pay for the correct service once than to book a short slot and still end up with half the job left.

*Public provider examples in 2026 show deep cleaning as a heavier service tier, often with team-based staffing, specialist tools, or an in-app quote rather than one flat national rate.*

## Rough 2026 price anchors

For decision-making, the safest way to think about pricing is by service model, not by one fixed national number.

- **Standard app-based home cleaning:** commonly around **170,000-250,000 VND** for **2 hours** and around **165,000-324,000 VND** for **3 hours** on public provider references, while some **4-hour** bookings can run higher depending on platform and pricing structure
- **Recurring cleaning:** often lands in a similar visit-level band, with better value when the same home is booked on a repeat schedule rather than as a rush one-off

These numbers are most useful as **budget anchors**, not as guaranteed quotes.

For most expats, these are the practical starting points:

## What to confirm before the cleaner arrives

This matters more than most first-time bookings expect.

Confirm these points in advance:
- the exact rooms and tasks
- whether dishes, ironing, linen changes, balcony cleaning, or inside-fridge work are included
- whether the cleaner brings tools and chemicals for this service type
- whether building access rules limit evening entry, service lift use, or parking
- whether someone needs to stay in the apartment during the session
- whether pets are in the home
- the final in-app price, holiday surcharge, and payment method

For first-time app bookings, it also helps to leave short task notes and room photos where the app supports it. If Vietnamese ability is limited, that step can matter more than a long text explanation. [Vietnam Essential Apps](/vietnam/daily-life/vietnam-essential-apps-2026) is useful if the household is still building its local app setup.

## Which option fits which renter

### Studio or one-bedroom renter
Start with a normal app-based cleaner. A short session is usually enough unless the apartment has been neglected for a while.

### Busy couple in a two-bedroom apartment
Recurring cleaning often makes more sense than waiting for the mess to build up and rebooking each time.

### Family home or pet home
Recurring cleaning is usually the base solution, with a heavier deep clean from time to time when the home needs a reset.

### Move-in, move-out, or handover cleanup
Book deep cleaning first. That is the cleaner way to reset the apartment before treating it as a routine-maintenance home.

### Short-stay serviced apartment renter
Check the included cleaning schedule before booking outside help. Paying twice for the same service is common when this step gets skipped.

## FAQ

### Can the cleaner be expected to speak English?

Not automatically. Some booking flows are easier for expats than others, but cleaner-side communication still varies. Short task notes, room-by-room lists, and photos usually work better than assuming spoken English will be enough.

### Do cleaners bring their own supplies?

Sometimes yes, sometimes only for specific service types. Deep-cleaning packages are more likely to include equipment and chemicals. Standard cleaning should be confirmed before the booking starts.

### Is an independent cleaner better than an app?

It can be, but usually only after there is a trusted referral. For a first booking, the app route is often easier because the quote, timing, profile, and support path are clearer.

### Should laundry, sofa cleaning, or AC cleaning be bundled into the same booking?

Do not assume that. Those are often separate services or separate charges. Laundry is usually its own workflow, and AC cleaning is a specialist household-maintenance task rather than part of normal home cleaning.
