## The short answer

Food delivery works well for many expats in Vietnam's main cities, but the reliable setup is not just “install an app.” Start with GrabFood and ShopeeFood, keep beFood and Xanh SM Ngon as secondary checks, use a reachable phone number, save a clear apartment address, keep cash as backup, and compare the final checkout total before ordering.

For most new arrivals in Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City / Saigon, and Da Nang, the first-week setup should be:

- install Grab and ShopeeFood
- add a Vietnamese SIM number if possible
- save the exact building name, tower, street number, and lobby handoff note
- keep small cash notes for orders where cash is available
- test one low-risk order before relying on delivery during rain, work calls, or late nights

Food delivery is convenient in dense urban areas, but it still depends on restaurant coverage, driver supply, weather, payment method, and whether the rider can actually find the building.

## Which food delivery apps should expats use first?

### GrabFood

GrabFood is often the easiest first app for new arrivals to check in major cities, especially for readers already using Grab for rides. ShopeeFood may be just as useful, or better, for nearby local choices and promotions in some districts or cities.

Use GrabFood first when:

- the address is in a central district or dense residential area
- the reader already has a Grab account working for transport
- the order is from a known chain, mall restaurant, or central-city merchant
- the priority is predictable app flow rather than finding the cheapest possible meal

Watchouts:

- restaurant availability depends on the exact address
- the same restaurant may appear at one address and disappear at another
- total cost includes food price plus delivery fee and possible app-level charges
- cash may be available at checkout, but it should not be assumed for every account or order

### ShopeeFood

ShopeeFood is the other core app most expats should check. It can be strong for local restaurants, drinks, snacks, and promotions, especially in large cities.

Use ShopeeFood when:

- GrabFood has limited nearby options
- the reader wants more local food choices
- drink, snack, or casual meal options matter
- promotions change the final price enough to compare

Watchouts:

- checkout payment options can vary by account and order
- app estimates are not delivery promises
- cancellation can be harder once the restaurant has accepted or prepared the order
- the interface and support flow may feel less familiar to new arrivals

### beFood

beFood is worth checking as a secondary app where it shows useful nearby restaurants. It should not be treated as the main default for every foreigner, but it can add options in cities where Be already has good rider coverage.

Use beFood when:

- GrabFood and ShopeeFood both show weak nearby coverage
- the reader already uses Be for rides
- the app shows enough restaurants near the saved address

Watchouts:

- coverage can be more uneven
- public consumer information is less clear than for GrabFood and ShopeeFood
- the best use case may depend heavily on city and district

### Xanh SM Ngon

Xanh SM Ngon is a newer option to check in supported areas, especially Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City / Saigon. It should be treated as a secondary app, not as a universal replacement for GrabFood or ShopeeFood.

Use Xanh SM Ngon when:

- the app shows good nearby restaurants
- the reader already uses Xanh SM for transport
- the delivery area is clearly supported

Watchouts:

- coverage is still changing
- restaurant choice may be narrower than the leading apps
- usefulness may differ sharply by neighborhood

## The practical app hierarchy

For most expats, the order of checking is:

1. **GrabFood** — often the easiest first check for new arrivals already using Grab.
2. **ShopeeFood** — often just as useful for local choice and promotions.
3. **beFood** — useful if coverage is good near the address.
4. **Xanh SM Ngon** — worth checking in supported areas, but still secondary for most readers.

Older Vietnam food-delivery recommendations that still mention Baemin, GoFood / Gojek, or Loship should be treated as stale for 2026 food delivery.

This is not a permanent ranking. Food delivery changes by city, district, promotion cycle, and restaurant participation. The correct app is the one that shows a reliable restaurant, a usable payment option, a reasonable ETA, and a final price that still makes sense.

## Payment reality: cash, cards, wallets, and checkout friction

The payment method shown at checkout is the only payment method that matters for that order. Platform pages may describe several payment options, but the app can still limit what appears based on account, merchant, order value, promotion, or current rules.

A practical setup:

- **Cash:** useful backup, especially in the first week. Keep small notes because riders may not have change for large notes.
- **Foreign card:** may work in some cases, but should be tested before depending on it.
- **Local wallet or bank-linked payment:** useful for longer stays, but not always realistic for new arrivals.
- **Promo-linked payment:** can lower the total, but may require a specific wallet, card, or account condition.

Do not place a food order assuming a foreign card will work just because the same card worked for a hotel, airline, or ride-hailing order. Card success can vary by issuer, app, account setup, and checkout flow.

For readers still building a backup spending setup for Vietnam, a [Wise multi-currency card](https://wise.com/invite/dic/ganqiuh) can help with foreign-card spending and ATM backup, but it does not remove app-specific checkout limits. Availability and fees depend on the reader's Wise account country and card terms. Food delivery still needs a cash or local-payment fallback.

For a broader payment setup, see [International Cards in Vietnam](/vietnam/banking/international-cards-vietnam-2026).

## Do expats need a Vietnamese SIM?

A Vietnamese SIM is not always the same as a formal app requirement, but it is one of the easiest ways to reduce delivery failure. Riders may call to confirm the location, ask about substitutions, find the building entrance, or say they have arrived.

A foreign number can be awkward because:

- riders may not want to call an international number
- OTP and account verification can be less reliable
- building guards or reception desks may not recognize the contact
- missed calls can cause delays or cancellation risk

For food delivery, the safest setup is a Vietnamese SIM with mobile data and the same number saved in the delivery app. For setup options, see [Vietnam SIM Card Guide](/vietnam/daily-life/vietnam-sim-card-guide-2026).

## How to enter an address so the rider can find it

Many failed food deliveries are address problems, not restaurant problems.

For apartments, enter the address in layers:

1. building or compound name
2. tower / block
3. street number and street name
4. ward / district if the app asks
5. lobby or reception handoff point
6. phone number that the rider can call

Example delivery note:

> Please leave at lobby reception. Building A, Tower 2. Call on arrival.

If a Vietnamese note is useful, keep it short:

> Gửi lễ tân sảnh giúp em. Tòa A, tháp 2. Gọi khi tới.

For alleys, small streets, or local houses, add the nearest landmark. App pins can be wrong by one entrance, one alley, or one side of a large apartment complex.

For broader handoff habits that also apply to parcels, see [Receiving Parcels in Vietnam](/vietnam/daily-life/receive-parcels-online-orders-vietnam-2026).

## What happens when the rider calls in Vietnamese?

Assume the rider may call in Vietnamese. The call is usually about one of five things:

- the restaurant is out of an item
- the rider cannot find the building
- the rider is at a different gate
- the rider has arrived and cannot wait long
- the payment or cash change needs confirmation

Useful short replies:

- “At the lobby.” — `Em ở sảnh.`
- “Please leave it at reception.” — `Gửi lễ tân giúp em.`
- “Please call when you arrive.” — `Tới nơi gọi giúp em.`
- “No Vietnamese. Please text.” — `Em không nói tiếng Việt. Nhắn tin giúp em.`
- “Coming down now.” — `Em xuống ngay.`

Keep the phone nearby after ordering. If the rider cannot reach the buyer, the order can become harder to fix.

## Costs: what to budget for delivery fees and app charges

Food delivery costs are live-checkout data, not fixed tariffs. The final total can change by distance, demand, weather, small-order rules, app fees, promotions, and payment method.

As a cautious 2026 checkout-planning range, many ordinary short urban meal orders may show delivery and platform-related charges in the **15,000-60,000 VND** range before food price and optional tips. This is not an official tariff, fixed fee, or safe national average. Short central orders with promotions can be lower. Longer-distance orders, rain, lunch and dinner peaks, or small-order rules can push the total higher.

Check three numbers before ordering:

- the food subtotal
- the delivery / service / platform-related charges
- the final price after promotion

Also compare the app menu price with the walk-in price when the difference matters. Some restaurants price delivery differently, and promotions can hide that difference until checkout.

*Source note: Delivery fees, service charges, promotions, and payment options are volatile. Treat the app checkout screen as the current source for that order.*

## Timing: when delivery is fast and when it fails

For nearby city orders, a cautious planning range is **20-45 minutes** in normal conditions and **45-75+ minutes** during rain, peak lunch or dinner, long-distance orders, large apartment handoffs, or restaurant delays.

Use delivery when:

- the restaurant is nearby
- the ETA is reasonable before checkout
- the rider can reach the address easily
- the payment method is already tested

Avoid relying on delivery when:

- heavy rain has started
- the order is needed before a meeting
- the address is hard to find
- the phone is low on battery
- the app shows a long ETA before checkout
- the order is from a restaurant that often substitutes or cancels items

If the restaurant is less than 5-10 minutes away on foot, takeaway can be faster than waiting for a rider.

## What to do if an order goes wrong

If an order is late, missing items, wrong, or marked delivered but not received, handle it inside the app first.

A practical order-problem flow:

1. check the app status and driver location
2. call or message the rider only for location or handoff issues
3. take photos if items are missing, damaged, or clearly wrong
4. use the app help/support flow
5. avoid cancelling casually after the restaurant has accepted or prepared the order
6. do not make side agreements outside the app for refunds or replacement unless the app records the change

Refunds and credits are not guaranteed. Outcomes can depend on payment method, order stage, merchant preparation, evidence, account history, and the platform decision.

## When direct restaurant ordering works better

Some restaurants in Vietnam still handle orders through Facebook, Zalo, phone, or their own delivery staff. This can work well for familiar restaurants, small neighborhood shops, or places that are not active on delivery apps.

Direct ordering is better when:

- the restaurant is nearby and already known
- the menu on the app looks incomplete
- the app price is much higher than the restaurant price
- the restaurant has clear Zalo or Facebook ordering instructions
- the buyer can communicate in Vietnamese or use simple translated messages

App ordering is safer when:

- the restaurant is new to the reader
- payment tracking matters
- delivery tracking matters
- the address is difficult
- support or refund documentation may be needed

## Where grocery and convenience delivery fit

Food delivery apps can blur into drinks, convenience items, and groceries. For meals, use the food-delivery workflow above. For supermarket-style orders, water, household items, or bulk grocery runs, dedicated grocery channels may work better.

## First-week setup checklist

Before relying on food delivery in Vietnam, set up:

- Grab and ShopeeFood
- one secondary app: beFood or Xanh SM Ngon, depending on city coverage
- Vietnamese SIM or reachable phone number
- saved apartment address with tower, lobby, and handoff note
- small cash notes
- one tested card or wallet option
- a short Vietnamese note for reception handoff
- a backup nearby restaurant for pickup

The practical rule is simple: use the app, but do not depend on the app alone. Food delivery works best when the phone, payment, address, rider communication, and building handoff are already solved.
